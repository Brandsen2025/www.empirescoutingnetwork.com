Player Overview

Roberto "El Cóndor" Rojas was a goalkeeper of immense natural talent. He possessed elite reflexes (SAV), agility (DYN), and a commanding presence that made him a dominant force for Colo-Colo, São Paulo, and the Chilean national team. However, his profile is irrevocably defined by the 1989 "Maracanazo" incident, where he faked an injury from a firework, leading to a lifetime ban. This act represents one of the most extreme examples of negative CHR (Character) and CRS (Clutch Response) in FGA history, overshadowing his otherwise world-class abilities.
