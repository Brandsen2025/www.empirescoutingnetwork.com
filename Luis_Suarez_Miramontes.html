Player Overview

Luis Suárez Miramontes, "El Arquitecto," was the defining deep-lying playmaker of his generation. His game was built on flawless technique (DXT), supreme elegance (ELE), and unparalleled vision (KEY, LGP). He dictated the tempo of matches with effortless composure (CPX) and pinpoint long-range passing. As the creative fulcrum for both Barcelona and Helenio Herrera's "Grande Inter," he combined attacking flair with the tactical intelligence (POI) to control the game from deep, earning the 1960 Ballon d'Or.
