Quick Summary

Player Overview

Vinnie Jones was a defensive midfielder whose game was built on pure physical presence and intimidation. He was not a technical player; rather, he was an enforcer, a "hard man" who specialized in a "rough playing style" and "continuously fouling opponents". As a pivotal member of Wimbledon's "Crazy Gang," he provided the steel, aggression (high AGG), and leadership (high LEA) that led them to their historic 1988 FA Cup victory over Liverpool. His career was defined by his fearsome reputation and physical dominance.
