Quick Summary

A Colombian forward famed for his incredible combination of height (1.88m), blistering pace, and dribbling ability. A cult hero in the FIFA video game series, Ibarbo's peak came at Cagliari in Serie A, earning him a spot on Colombia's 2014 World Cup team and moves to clubs like AS Roma and Watford.

Player Overview

Víctor Ibarbo was a unique and electrifying attacking player. At his peak with Cagliari, he was a tactical nightmare for defenders, possessing the height of a target man (1.88m) with the elite pace and dribbling skills of a top-tier winger. This rare combination made him a legend in the *FIFA* video game series and a valuable asset in real life, allowing him to play as a second striker or on either flank. His Serie A form (130 appearances) led him to Colombia's 2014 World Cup squad and subsequent moves to AS Roma and Watford.

Strengths and Weaknesses

Strengths

  • Pace (PAC): Blistering, game-changing speed, especially for his height.
  • Physicality (PHY): At 1.88m, he was incredibly strong and difficult to knock off the ball.
  • Dribbling (DRI): A powerful and effective dribbler who "likes to dribble".
  • Versatility: Could play as a central striker, second striker, or on either wing.

Weaknesses

  • Finishing (FIN): Not a clinical or prolific goal-scorer; his end product was his main weakness.
  • Discipline: Prone to committing fouls and had a poor disciplinary record.
  • Ball Retention: His dribbling style meant he was often fouled but also had a tendency to lose the ball.
