Player Overview

Just "Justo" Fontaine is the owner of one of football's most "unbreakable" records: 13 goals in a single World Cup (1958). He was a "complete" and "clinical" striker, a master of movement, pace, and two-footed finishing. He scored in every single match of the 1958 tournament, including a hat-trick on his debut and 4 goals in the 3rd place match vs. West Germany. At club level, he was a superstar for Stade de Reims (replacing Raymond Kopa), winning 3 French titles and finishing as the 1958-59 European Cup top scorer. His career was tragically cut short by a leg injury at just 28, but his 1958 "peak" remains one of the greatest goalscoring feats in history.
