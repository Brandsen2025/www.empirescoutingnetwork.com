Player Overview

Manuel "Garrincha" Francisco dos Santos was pure, unfiltered genius. His game was defined by one thing: generational dribbling (DXT, FLA 10.0). Possessing a famous physical ailment (bent legs), he used it to his advantage with a devastating, trademark feint that was famously unstoppable, even when defenders knew it was coming. He combined this skill with explosive pace (SPR) and creativity (CRK), dismantling defenses for fun. He was the star of the 1962 World Cup, winning the Golden Ball & Golden Boot. His profile is one of supreme talent offset by a complete lack of professionalism (low CHR, CON), which shortened his peak.
