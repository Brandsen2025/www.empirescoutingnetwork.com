Quick Summary

A true "one-club man," Miguel Ángel Russo was an iconic defensive midfielder for Estudiantes de La Plata, playing his entire 14-year career (1975-1988) there. He was the on-field general for the team that won back-to-back Argentine titles (1982 Metropolitano, 1983 Nacional). He later became one of the most respected and successful managers in South American history, famously guiding Boca Juniors to the 2007 Copa Libertadores.

Player Overview

Miguel Ángel Russo was the epitome of a club legend. A tough, intelligent, and tireless defensive midfielder, he was the heart and soul of Estudiantes de La Plata for his entire 14-year playing career. He was a key component and on-field leader of Carlos Bilardo's championship-winning side, capturing consecutive titles in 1982 and 1983. His reliability and intelligence earned him 17 caps for the Argentina national team. While his playing career was defined by loyalty, his managerial career was defined by continental glory, most notably winning the Copa Libertadores with Boca Juniors in 2007 and multiple titles with Rosario Central.
