Quick Summary

One of the greatest players in Chilean history. Sánchez was a "warrior" with a "divine left foot." He was the star of the **1962 World Cup**, winning the **Golden Boot** (top scorer) and **Bronze Ball** (3rd best player) while leading Chile to a historic 3rd place finish. At club level, he was the icon of the legendary **"Ballet Azul"** (Blue Ballet) team at Universidad de Chile, winning 6 league titles.

Player Overview

Leonel "El León" Sánchez was a player of fierce contrasts. He was the main star of the "Ballet Azul" (Blue Ballet), an iconic Universidad de Chile team named for its elegant play, yet he was also a ferocious competitor. He possessed one of the most powerful left-foot shots in history, a "divine" weapon he used for open-play goals and free-kicks (famously beating Lev Yashin in 1962). His defining moment was the 1962 World Cup on home soil. He won the Golden Boot and led Chile to a 3rd place finish, but he also cemented his "warrior" legend in the infamous "Battle of Santiago," where he famously punched an Italian player. He was a complete attacker: fast, technical, a great finisher, and mentally tougher than anyone on the pitch.
