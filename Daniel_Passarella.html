Player Overview

Daniel Passarella, "El Kaiser," was a revolutionary defender. At only 5' 8", he was one of the most dominant aerial players in the world, renowned for his timing and "hanging" leap. He was a complete "Libero": a ferocious, intimidating tackler and a supreme leader, but also a sophisticated attacking weapon. He possessed a powerful shot, was a free-kick specialist, and relentlessly joined the attack, scoring 175 goals in his career—a record for a defender for many years. He is most famous for being the captain and emotional center of the 1978 World Cup-winning Argentina team. He is an eternal legend at River Plate, where he won 7 league titles.
