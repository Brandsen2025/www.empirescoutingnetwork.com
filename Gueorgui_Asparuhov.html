Player Overview

Gueorgui "Gundi" Asparuhov was a "mythological" figure, the "James Dean" of Bulgarian football. He was a "total" forward who possessed a rare combination of height, speed, sublime technical skill, and aerial dominance. He was a "one-club man" at heart for Levski Sofia, becoming the ultimate icon of the club. In 1965, he was named Bulgarian Footballer of the Year and finished 8th in the Ballon d'Or. He famously starred for Bulgaria in the 1966 World Cup, scoring their only goal of the tournament. His legend was cemented by his tragic death in a car accident in 1971 at just 28, an event that is a national day of mourning for Levski fans.
