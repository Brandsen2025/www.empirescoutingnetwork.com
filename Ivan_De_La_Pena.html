Player Overview

Iván de la Peña López, known as "Lo Pelat" (The Bald One), was a Spanish international midfielder and one of the most enigmatic playmakers of his generation. A product of La Masia, he was hailed as the "heir to Guardiola" but played with the "flair of Laudrup." His "generational" vision allowed him to see and execute passes that no other player could. He was a key part of Bobby Robson's Barcelona, forming a legendary partnership with Ronaldo and winning the 1997 UEFA Cup Winners' Cup and 1997 Copa del Rey. After a spell at Lazio, he returned to Barcelona and became an "eternal idol" at city rivals RCD Espanyol, where he was the creative "brain" for nine seasons, winning another Copa del Rey in 2006.
