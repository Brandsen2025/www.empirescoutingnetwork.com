Quick Summary

The "Knight of Football," Masopust is a legend of the game and the first Czech player to win the **1962 Ballon d'Or**. He was an elegant, hard-working, and brilliant "box-to-box" midfielder. He led Czechoslovakia to the **1962 World Cup Final**, where he scored the opening goal against Brazil. He was famed for his "Masopust Slalom" (dribbling) and his supreme sportsmanship.

Player Overview

Josef Masopust was one of the most complete midfielders of his generation. He was the "brain" and "engine" of the great Dukla Prague (8x league titles) and Czechoslovakia teams. His style was a unique blend of "worker" and "artist." He had the stamina of a box-to-box midfielder but the technical grace of a #10, famed for his "Masopust Slalom"—a mesmerizing, elegant dribbling run. His peak came in 1962, when he led Czechoslovakia to the World Cup final (scoring their only goal) and was subsequently awarded the Ballon d'Or. He was also a "Knight" of the game, famous for his sportsmanship (e.g., checking on an injured Pelé in 1962 rather than playing on).
