Player Overview

Cafú is widely regarded as one of the greatest full-backs of all time, defined by relentless stamina, rapid overlapping runs and elite crossing. He combined defensive reliability with elite forward contribution and captained Brazil to the 2002 World Cup title, embodying modern attacking full-back archetypes across club and international football.

Career Timeline

Youth and Brazil: rose through São Paulo youth system and established himself in the early 1990s. Short European spell at Zaragoza (1994–95), returned to Brazil, then key move to Roma (1997–2003) where he won a Serie A title. Joined AC Milan (2003–2008), adding domestic and continental honours and appearing in the 2005 Champions League final. International: Brazil (1990–2006), three World Cup finals (1994, 1998, 2002) and two World Cup wins.

International Career

Cafú is Brazil's most‑capped outfield player and the only player to appear in three consecutive World Cup finals, winning in 1994 and 2002 and captaining the 2002 winning side. Tactical Philosophies & DNA

Cafú's tactical DNA suits high-tempo, transitional teams that rely on full-backs for width and vertical overloads. He excels in systems that permit sustained overlapping, quick outlet passing, and dynamic defensive recovery. High fit with Brazilian attacking wing-back traditions and modern Klopp/Guardiola transitional demands when combined with disciplined centre-back partners.
