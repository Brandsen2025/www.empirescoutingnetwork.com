Player Overview

Paulo Roberto Falcão was the definition of "on-field intelligence." A complete midfielder, he was neither a #10 nor a defensive anchor, but the "Regista" who did everything. He was the conductor of the legendary 1982 Brazil team, a "coach on the field" who dictated the tempo with his flawless technique, 360-degree vision, and pinpoint passing. At Internacional, he was the star of their undefeated 1979 Brazilian championship. He then moved to AS Roma, where he was nicknamed "O Rei de Roma" (The King of Rome) after leading them to the 1982-83 Serie A title. Falcão was a player who made the entire team better; a cerebral, elegant, and timeless legend.

Strengths and Weaknesses

Strengths

  • Tactical IQ (IQ): A "genius." His understanding of space and time was perfect.
  • Vision & Passing (VIS/PAS): A "Regista" who controlled the entire game. Flawless passing range.
  • Technique (TEC): Sublime first touch and ball control. Played with his head up.
  • Leadership (LDR): A calm, authoritative "coach on the field."

Weaknesses

  • Pace: Was famously unhurried. Relied on his brain, not his legs.
  • Defensive Tackling: Was not a ball-winner or "enforcer." Relied on positioning.
  • World Cup "What If": Part of the "greatest team to *not* win the World Cup" (1982).
