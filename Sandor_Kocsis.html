Quick Summary

"Golden Head," Kocsis was the most prolific aerial goalscorer in football history. He was the "finisher" of the legendary **"Mighty Magyars"** (Hungary) and won the **1954 World Cup Golden Boot** with a record 11 goals. A legend for Honvéd, he later fled to Spain and became a star for **FC Barcelona**, winning two La Liga titles.

Player Overview

Sándor Kocsis, "Aranyfej" (Golden Head), was a goalscoring phenomenon. While Puskás was the "General" and Hidegkuti the "False 9," Kocsis was the "Executioner" of the "Mighty Magyars." He was, without argument, the greatest header of the ball in football history. His performance at the 1954 World Cup is legendary: he won the Golden Boot with 11 goals, including two hat-tricks, and scored 2 goals in extra time to defeat Uruguay in the semi-final. He scored 75 goals in 68 caps for Hungary (a 1.10 goals/game average). After the 1956 Hungarian Revolution, he fled to Spain and became a legend for FC Barcelona, winning two league titles and two Fairs Cups.
