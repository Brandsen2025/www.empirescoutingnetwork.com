Player Overview

Zinedine Zidane, 'Zizou', was footballing artistry personified. He played the game with a unique blend of grace, power, and intelligence (elite ELE, DXT, POI). His signature first touch, 'Roulette' turns, and ability to shield the ball under pressure were unparalleled (high CPX, Press Resistance). As a playmaker, his vision and passing range (KEY, LGP) dictated play, but he was equally known for scoring decisive, spectacular goals in the most important moments (high CRS), including World Cup and Champions League finals.

Strengths and Weaknesses

Strengths

  • Technique & First Touch (DXT/ELE): Generational; arguably the best first touch in history.
  • Composure & Press Resistance (CPX): Seemingly impossible to dispossess; elegant turns ('Roulette').
  • Vision & Passing (KEY/LGP): Masterful playmaker, saw and executed any pass.
  • Big Game Performer (CRS): Scored in two World Cup finals and a CL final.
  • Dribbling & Ball Control (CRK/FLA): Gliding, powerful dribbler who manipulated the ball at will.

Weaknesses

  • Pace (SPR): Relied on grace, power, and skill rather than pure explosive speed.
  • Defensive Work Rate (TPI/MRK): Not a high-volume presser; contributed positionally.
  • Temperament: Prone to moments of intense reaction (e.g., 2006 WC Final).
