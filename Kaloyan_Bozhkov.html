Player Overview

Kaloyan Bozhkov is a rare profile: a deep-lying midfielder who dictates play with the offensive output of an advanced #10. He first gained prominence as the captain of the Bulgaria U-17s, but his 2024-25 season with Lokomotiva Zagreb's U-19s was his true breakout. From a defensive midfield position, he scored 8 goals and added over 10 assists in 25 games, leading them to a league title. This phenomenal ability to "pull the strings" and "read the game superbly" caught the eye of Dinamo Zagreb's president and club legend, Zvonimir Boban, who personally insisted on signing him. He is the first Bulgarian player to join Dinamo, with local media comparing his technical, creative profile to that of fellow Dinamo academy product Dani Olmo.

Core Tactical DNA

Playing Style: A modern "Regista" or deep-lying playmaker. His DNA is that of a #10 playing in a #6 position. He controls the entire tempo of the game, using his superb reading of the game and technical passing ability to "pull the strings." His value is not in his tackling, but in his ability to start attacks and break lines from deep. His incredible goal and assist record shows a player who also has the intelligence to join the attack, similar to the Dani Olmo comparison.
