Player Overview

Silvio Piola was the "Gerd Müller" of his generation, a "pure" and "prolific" goal machine. His record of 274 Serie A goals has stood for over 70 years and is unlikely to ever be broken. Piola was a "complete" forward: he was big, strong, fast, and could score with both feet, but he was most famous for his "acrobatic" finishing, including bicycle kicks. His defining moment came in the 1938 World Cup, where he was the star striker for the winning Italian team, scoring 5 goals, including two in the final against Hungary. He was a legend for multiple clubs, most notably Pro Vercelli, Lazio, and Novara.
