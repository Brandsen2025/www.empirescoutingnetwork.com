Player Overview

Oleg Blokhin is one of Eastern Europe's all‑time great forwards, famed for electric pace, lethal finishing and relentless solo threats. The archetypal Lobanovskyi-era Dynamo Kyiv attacker, Blokhin combined athletic acceleration with intelligent movement and clinical decision-making to dominate the Soviet Top League and produce consistent performances at club and international level.

Career Timeline

1969–1988: Dynamo Kyiv (dominant domestic and continental years). 1988–1989: Vorwärts Steyr (Austria). 1989–1990: Aris Limassol (Cyprus). International: Soviet Union (1972–1988). Transitioned to management after retirement, including successful spells in Greece and multiple appointments with the Ukraine national team.

International Career

Blokhin is the Soviet Union's most capped player and joint top scorer in many sources, representing the USSR across World Cups and Olympics. He brought pace and directness to the national side and produced important goals across major tournaments.
