Player Overview

Malher Tressor Moreno Baldrich was a well-traveled Colombian attacking midfielder, renowned for his technical skill and playmaking. He made a sensational professional debut in Peru with Alianza Lima in 1999, scoring 16 goals in 40 games. This led to a move to Atlético Nacional, where he won the 2000 Copa Merconorte. He had several spells in Europe with FC Metz, but found his greatest consistency in Mexico, becoming a key creative force for Necaxa and San Luis, for whom he played over 110 matches. He also played for the Colombia national team 32 times, scoring 7 goals.
