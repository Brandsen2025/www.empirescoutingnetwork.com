Quick Summary

A "cult hero" of Italian football, Giovanni Rocotelli was a maverick winger and technical pioneer. While a journeyman player largely in Serie B and C, his global legacy is immense. He is credited as the "father of the rabona" (*incrociata*), being the first player to ever perform the move consistently and, crucially, the first to have it captured on television (1978).

Player Overview

Giovanni Rocotelli is a true football innovator. While others (like Infante) may have used the rabona in unfilmed matches, Rocotelli was the one who perfected it, weaponized it, and introduced it to the world. He was a pacy, skillful right winger who famously used the rabona *as his primary method of crossing the ball*. His most famous moment, and the move's "birth" on television, came in 1978 for Ascoli. This act of audacity cemented his status as "Mister Rabona" and a cult legend whose technical contribution to the game far outshines his modest playing career.
