Player Overview

Javier Zanetti is an Argentine icon and the ultimate "one-club man" for Inter Milan. He is, simply put, a legend of professionalism and longevity. Nicknamed "Il Trattore" (The Tractor) for his tireless stamina and relentless work rate, Zanetti played an astonishing 858 games for Inter over 19 seasons, captaining the side for 13 years. He was incredibly versatile, starting his career as a marauding full-back and seamlessly transitioning into a world-class defensive midfielder. His career culminated in 2010 when, as captain, he lifted the UEFA Champions League, completing an unprecedented Italian treble under José Mourinho. He retired at age 40, and his #4 shirt was retired by the club.
