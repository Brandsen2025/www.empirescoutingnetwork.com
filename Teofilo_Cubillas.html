Player Overview

Teófilo "El Nene" Cubillas is a national hero in Peru and one of the greatest attacking midfielders in football history. He was the talisman for Peru's "golden generation," leading them to a 1975 Copa América title and two World Cup quarter-finals (1970, 1978). His game was defined by exceptional goal-scoring (high FIN), creative flair, and one of the most famous free-kick techniques ever (high FRK). He scored 10 goals in 13 World Cup matches, making him the top-scoring midfielder in the tournament's history. Pelé famously called Cubillas his "successor" after the 1970 World Cup.

  • Free Kicks (FRK): World-class specialist. Famed for his powerful, audacious "toe-poke" and outside-of-the-boot strikes.
  • Technique & Dribbling (TEC): Exceptional technical skill, often caressing the ball with the outside of his foot.
  • Big Game Player (BGP): A true World Cup legend; 10 goals in 13 WC matches.
  • Vision (VIS): A classic #10 playmaker who could create and score.

Weaknesses

  • Defensive Work Rate: A classic "fantasista," exempt from defensive duties.
  • Physicality: Relied on skill and intelligence over strength.
  • Aerial Ability: At 1.73m, was not an aerial threat.
