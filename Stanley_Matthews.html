Player Overview

Sir Stanley Matthews was football's first global superstar and the definitive "gentleman" of the game. He was the "Wizard of the Dribble," a player of generational technical skill (DXT 10.0), balance, and body feints. His game was built on beating his man from a standing start (PAC 9.5) and delivering a perfect cross (KEY 9.7). His professionalism and fitness were legendary, allowing him to play at the top level until age 50 and win the FWA Footballer of the Year at 48. He was the inaugural winner of the Ballon d'Or in 1956.
