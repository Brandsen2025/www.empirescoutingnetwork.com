Quick Summary

A Brazilian icon who redefined the perception of goalkeepers in his country. Taffarel was not tall (1.82m) but compensated with explosive **Reflexes (REF)** and legendary penalty-saving ability. He anchored Brazil to the 1994 World Cup title and is now a world-renowned goalkeeping coach.

Player Overview

Cláudio Taffarel proved that height isn't everything for a goalkeeper. His **Reflexes (REF)** were cat-like, and his **Clutch Response Score (CRS)** is legendary due to his 1994 World Cup Final and 1998 Semi-Final shootout heroics. He played with a technical style (using his feet) that was rare for the 90s, influencing the modern Brazilian school of goalkeeping (Alisson, Ederson).

Strengths and Weaknesses

Strengths

  • **Penalty Saving (PEN):** Psychological dominance in shootouts (10.0).
  • **Reflexes (REF):** Explosive reaction speed to close-range shots.
  • **Composure:** Played with a smile; extreme calm under pressure.
  • **Footwork:** Comfortable with the ball, pioneering for his era.

Weaknesses

  • **Aerial Crosses:** Struggled occasionally with high crosses in crowded boxes due to height.

Global Impact Analysis

**Legacy:** Opened the door for Brazilian goalkeepers in Europe (Dida, Julio Cesar, Alisson). Before him, Brazilian keepers were not trusted.

**Coaching:** Now shapes the best keepers in the world (Alisson at Liverpool) as a coach.

**FGA Value:** The ultimate "Clutch" goalkeeper. Value spikes in knockout tournaments.
