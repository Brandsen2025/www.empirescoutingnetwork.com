Player Overview

Tomás "El Trinche" Carlovich is a cult legend of Argentine football, celebrated for sublime ball mastery, improvised dribbles and an extraordinary passing range. Revered in Rosario and by peers across generations, Carlovich remained largely outside the top professional spotlight by choice, producing moments of genius in regional leagues that earned him near‑mythic status among coaches and fellow players.

Career Timeline

Youth at Rosario Central; multiple spells across Argentine clubs including Sporting de Bigand (loan), Central Córdoba (four periods), Independiente Rivadavia, Colón, Deportivo Maipú, Andes Talleres, Juventud de Pergamino and late career in regional sides. Retired in 1988; remained an iconic local figure until his death in 2020.
