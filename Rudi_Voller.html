Player Overview

Rudolf "Rudi" Völler is one of the most prolific and beloved strikers in German history, easily recognized by his iconic perm. He was a complete centre-forward, blending a tenacious work rate with "supreme" attacking position and lethal finishing. He was a 1990 World Cup champion with West Germany (drawing the decisive penalty in the final) and a 1993 Champions League winner with Marseille. He was a goal machine at Werder Bremen (97 goals in 137 games), winning German Footballer of the Year in 1983. He also starred for AS Roma, winning the 1991 Coppa Italia and was UEFA Cup top scorer.
