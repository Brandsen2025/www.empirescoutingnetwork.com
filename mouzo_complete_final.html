🏆 MAJOR ACHIEVEMENTS - 426 MATCHES RECORD

  • 🏆 426 Matches - ALL-TIME RECORD: Most appearances in Boca history. Record still stands 40+ years later. 396 in league, 30 in cups. Unbreakable loyalty mark.
  • 🏆 Copa Libertadores (2): 1977, 1978 - Captain for club's first-ever continental titles. Defensive anchor that established Boca as South American power.
  • 🏆 Copa Intercontinental 1977: World champion. Defeated Borussia Mönchengladbach. Captain who brought global glory to Boca.
  • 🏆 Metropolitano Championships (2): 1976, 1981. Defensive pillar in two title-winning campaigns separated by 5 years.
  • 🏆 Nacional Championship 1976: Domestic dominance. Best defender of the season award.
  • ⚡ 29 Superclásicos: Tied record with Marzolini. Most appearances in Argentina's biggest rivalry. Warrior in battles vs River.
  • 🎖️ 13 Seasons: 1971-1984. Nearly 14 years of continuous service. Youth academy product who became captain and legend.
  • 👑 Maradona's Choice: Diego Maradona named Mouzo as true figure of 1981 championship team. Ultimate respect from greatest player ever.

⭐ DEFINING MOMENTS

  • 🎯 Debut November 11, 1971: 0-0 draw vs Gimnasia y Esgrima (M). Started as #2 (right-back) during players' strike. Beginning of legendary 13-year journey.
  • 🏆 Copa Libertadores 1977: Captain for club's first-ever continental title. Scored 3 goals in tournament. Defensive anchor that revolutionized Boca's history.
  • 🌍 Intercontinental Cup 1977: December 11, Tokyo. Defeated Borussia Mönchengladbach. Captain who lifted Boca to world champion status. Historic moment.
  • 🏆 Copa Libertadores 1978: Back-to-back continental glory. Cemented Boca as South American powerhouse. Lorenzo's defensive system perfected.
  • 👑 Maradona's Recognition 1981: Diego named Mouzo as true figure of championship team. Greater honor than trophy itself - respect from GOAT.
  • 📊 Record Breaker December 11, 1983: Surpassed Silvio Marzolini's appearance record. Became most loyal player in Boca history. Legend status confirmed.
  • 💔 Final Match December 16, 1984: 2-0 win vs Rosario Central. Left due to conflicts with club executives, not choice. Bitter ending to glorious career.
  • 🔵 Return 2009: Carlos Bianchi offered him 4th division coaching role. Mouzo accepted, returned home after wife's death. Boca family forever.

He repeated the feat in 1978, then lifted the Intercontinental Cup. These weren't just trophies - they were revolutionary moments in club history, and Mouzo was the defensive anchor.

His 13 consecutive seasons (1971-1984) demonstrate remarkable consistency in an era with fewer matches per season. To accumulate 426 appearances required exceptional durability, availability, and sustained performance across more than a decade. Youth academy product to captain to all-time appearance leader - the complete one-club journey.

His famous quote 'Yo me hubiese muerto defendiendo a Boca ' (I would have died defending Boca) wasn't hyperbole - nearly literal truth. The club saved him when he needed saving.

Verdict: Elite defender (8.92/10) whose loyalty record defines his legacy more than any individual performance. Every club needs a Mouzo - warrior defender who bleeds club colors, leads by example, and gives entire career to the institution. In transfer-heavy modern football, his 13-season commitment and 426-match record represent dying breed of one-club legends."
