‚ñ∏ Career Summary

Pablo Alfaro is synonymous with grit, leadership, and the resurrection of Sevilla FC in the early 2000s. A tough-tackling centre-back, he held the record (alongside Xavi Aguado, later surpassed by Sergio Ramos) for the most red cards in La Liga history (18). However, reducing him to his disciplinary record ignores his vital role as the captain who transformed Sevilla from a Segunda Division side into a European contender.

Forming a fearsome partnership with Javi Navarro, Alfaro was the heart of a defense that no striker wanted to face. His intelligence off the pitch (he is a medical doctor) contrasted with his ruthless style on it. While he played for Zaragoza (winning the Copa del Rey), Barcelona, and Racing, his legacy is defined by his years in Seville, where he laid the foundations for the club's golden era of UEFA Cup dominance.
