Club Overview

Albion Football Club holds a legendary place in South American football as the oldest club in Uruguay, founded on June 1, 1891. Started as "Football Association" by students, it was renamed "Albion" to honor the birthplace of the sport. The club was a dominant force in the 1890s, playing in the first Uruguayan derby against CURCC (Peñarol) in 1892. After spending 113 years outside the top flight, the club achieved a historic promotion back to the Primera División in 2021.
