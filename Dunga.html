Player Overview

Dunga (Carlos Caetano Bledorn Verri) is one of the most significant figures in modern Brazilian football. He was a tough, pragmatic, and highly intelligent defensive midfielder, or "volante." While a cult hero at clubs like Fiorentina, his legacy was defined by the national team. After being blamed for the "un-Brazilian" 1990 team, he returned as captain in 1994, leading the nation to its first World Cup in 24 years. He was a pure "General," a master of the "Simeone Cholismo" philosophy, valued for his leadership, tackling, and simple, effective distribution.
