Quick Summary

The patriarch of the Álvarez dynasty, Quique Costas was a legendary Spanish defender and midfielder. Player Overview

Enrique "Quique" Álvarez Costas was a highly respected Spanish footballer, known for his defensive grit, versatility, and consistency. He began his career at Celta Vigo before a high-profile transfer to FC Barcelona in 1971. He became a key part of a legendary Barça team alongside Johan Cruyff, winning the 1973-74 La Liga title. He was a "utility" player in the best sense, able to perform at a high level as a centre-back, full-back, or defensive midfielder. He later became a long-time, respected coach at Barcelona's La Masia.
