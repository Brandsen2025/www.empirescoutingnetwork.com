Player Overview

Héctor "El Mago" Scarone was, by many accounts, the greatest player of his era. While his teammate José Andrade was the "total" midfielder, Scarone was the attacking genius. A small, supremely gifted inside-left, he was a master dribbler, a visionary passer, and a devastatingly accurate finisher. He formed a legendary partnership with Héctor Castro and Pedro Cea. Scarone was the star of the Uruguay team that dominated world football, winning the 1924 and 1928 Olympics (then the *de facto* world championship) and the inaugural 1930 FIFA World Cup. He famously scored the winning goal in the 1928 Olympic final and a key goal in the 1930 World Cup final. His 31 goals for Uruguay stood as a record until 2011.
