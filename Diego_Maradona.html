Player Overview

Diego Maradona is one of football’s defining geniuses, blending extraordinary dribbling, vision and creativity with an ability to dominate matches and single-handedly change outcomes; central to Napoli’s golden era and Argentina’s 1986 World Cup triumph.

Key Matches & Milestones

  • 1986 FIFA World Cup — tournament talisman: "Hand of God" and "Goal of the Century" vs England in quarter-final.
  • Led Napoli to first-ever Serie A titles (1986–87, 1989–90) and UEFA Cup 1989; transformed club status.
  • Named FIFA World Player of the Year and widely cited among the greatest ever; contested legacy with Pelé and Messi.
