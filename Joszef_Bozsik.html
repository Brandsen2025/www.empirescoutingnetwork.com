Player Overview

József Bozsik, "Cucu," was the "brain" and "conductor" of the "Mighty Magyars." While Puskás and Kocsis were the finishers, Bozsik was the deep-lying playmaker who dictated the entire game. He was a "Regista" decades before the term was coined, famed for his "flawless" technique, "genius" tactical intelligence, and "perfectly accurate" passing range. He was the "one-club" icon of Honvéd (along with Puskás) and the first Hungarian to reach 100 international caps. His "partnership" with the "false 9" (Hidegkuti) and "enforcer" (Zakariás) revolutionized midfield play in the 1950s.
