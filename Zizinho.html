Player Overview

Thomaz Soares da Silva, "Zizinho," was a Brazilian legend and the man Pelé himself called "the best player I ever saw." Nicknamed "Mestre Ziza," he was the complete footballer, a "harmonious blend of elegance and tenacity." He was an attacking midfielder who could do everything: "extraordinary dribbling," "precise passing," "keen footballing intelligence," and prolific goalscoring (146 goals for Flamengo). He was the undisputed star of the 1950 World Cup, winning the Golden Ball, but became a tragic hero in the "Maracanazo" loss. He also won the 1949 Copa América, where he was the all-time leader in goals (17) and assists (23) for decades.
