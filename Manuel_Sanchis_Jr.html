Player Overview

Manuel "Manolo" Sanchís Hontiyuelo is one of the most decorated and revered players in Real Madrid history. The son of 1966 European Cup hero Manuel Sanchís Martínez, Manolo was a "one-club man," playing 710 official matches for Real Madrid over 18 seasons. He was the defensive anchor of the legendary "La Quinta del Buitre" (Vulture's Cohort) that won 5 straight La Liga titles and 2 UEFA Cups in the 1980s. He later evolved into "El Capitán," the leader of the team that broke Madrid's 32-year drought to win the 1998 UEFA Champions League, and lifted the trophy again in 2000. He was an elegant, composed, and "generational" leader, a "libero" who read the game with "elite" intelligence.
