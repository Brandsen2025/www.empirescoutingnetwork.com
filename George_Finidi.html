Quick Summary

One of the greatest wingers of the 1990s, Finidi George was a key member of Louis van Gaal's legendary Ajax team. He famously won the 1995 UEFA Champions League, 3 Eredivisie titles, and the Intercontinental Cup. Renowned for his blistering pace (PAC), precise crossing (CRO), and tactical intelligence (IQ), he was also a star for Nigeria's "Golden Generation," winning the 1994 AFCON and playing in two World Cups.

Player Overview

Finidi George is a Nigerian football legend, widely considered one of the best wingers in the world during his peak. After playing for Nigerian clubs, he was signed by Ajax in 1993, where he became an integral part of Louis van Gaal's dominant "Total Football" revival. He provided the assist in the 1995 Champions League Final and formed a legendary partnership with Kanu. In 103 games for Ajax, he scored 18 goals and won 3 league titles and the UCL. He later starred for Real Betis, leading them to the Copa del Rey final. Internationally, he was a key figure in Nigeria's 1994 AFCON-winning team and played in the 1994 and 1998 World Cups.
