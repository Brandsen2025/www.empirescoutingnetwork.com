Quick Summary

A legendary Brazilian right-back and one of the most fearsome ball-strikers in football history. "O Canhão" was famed for his physics-defying, powerful "trivela" shots and free-kicks. His most iconic moment was his absurd, swerving goal against Italy in the 1978 World Cup. A 3-time Copa Libertadores winner (twice with Cruzeiro, once with Grêmio), he was a defender by name but a prolific, game-changing attacker in practice, scoring 105 goals for Cruzeiro alone.

Player Overview

Manoel Rezende de Mattos Cabral, known as Nelinho, was a Brazilian footballer who redefined the attacking full-back role. While others were defenders, Nelinho was a pure weapon. He possessed "generational" shooting power, earning him the nickname "O Canhão" (The Cannon). He was a master of the "trivela," able to bend the ball with the outside of his boot with ferocious pace and dip. This technique produced one of the most famous goals in history against Italy in the 1978 World Cup. He was a legend at Cruzeiro, scoring 105 goals and winning back-to-back Copa Libertadores titles. He later won a third Libertadores with Grêmio, cementing his status as one of South America's all-time greats.
