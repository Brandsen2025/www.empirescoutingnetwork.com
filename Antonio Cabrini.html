Player Overview

Antonio Cabrini is a defining modern full-back from Italy's golden generation — an attacking left-back whose timing, pace and left-foot delivery made him a decisive outlet for Juventus and the Italy national team. Winner of the 1982 FIFA World Cup and multiple continental trophies with Juventus, Cabrini combined defensive intelligence with genuine goal and assist threat, scoring nine international goals and earning the 1978 World Cup Best Young Player award.

Career Milestones & Highlights

1978: Best Young Player at the World Cup after helping Italy to fourth place and establishing himself internationally.

1982: World Cup winner; scored key goal vs Argentina in second round; missed a penalty in the final but Italy won.

1977–1986 (Juventus): Six Serie A titles and multiple European trophies; one of few players to win every major UEFA club competition.

Club captaincy: Inherited Juventus armband later in career, demonstrating leadership and standing at Turin.
