Player Overview

Enzo "El Príncipe" Francescoli is revered as one of the all-time great playmakers, a "silent maestro" whose elegant, "balletic" style inspired a generation (most notably, Zinedine Zidane). He was a "virtuoso" #10, defined by his grace, vision, and technique. He starred at River Plate, winning 5 Argentine titles and the 1996 Copa Libertadores. He also won Ligue 1 with Marseille and was twice named South American Footballer of the Year (1984, 1995). For Uruguay, he was a legend, winning three Copa América titles (1983, 1987, 1995) and playing in two World Cups.
