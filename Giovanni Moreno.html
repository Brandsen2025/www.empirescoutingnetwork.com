Player Overview

Giovanni "Gio" Moreno was one of the most unique playmakers of his generation. Standing at a massive 1.92m (6 ft 3 in), he possessed the sublime first touch, creative vision, and left-footed flair of a much smaller player. After rising from the Colombian 2nd tier with Envigado, he became a superstar at Atlético Nacional (34 goals in 67 apps) and a fan idol at Argentina's Racing Club. He spent the majority of his career as the captain and legend of Shanghai Shenhua, where he scored 70 goals and won two Chinese FA Cups.

Strengths and Weaknesses

Strengths

  • Technique (TEC): Exceptional skill, flair, and first touch for a player of his 1.92m stature.
  • Finishing (FIN): A prolific goal-scorer from midfield; 126 career goals.
  • Flair (FLA): A true "fantasista" capable of spectacular goals and backheels.
  • Aerial Ability (AER): His height made him a unique and dominant threat in the box.
  • Versatility: Could play as a #10, Second Striker, or Centre-Forward.

Weaknesses

  • Pace & Mobility: "El Flaco" was a languid, technical player, not an athlete.
  • Defensive Contribution: A classic #10 who offered very little defensively.
  • Consistency: His game was based on moments of genius rather than constant, high-tempo effort.
