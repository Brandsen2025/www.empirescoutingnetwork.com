Player Overview

Josef "Pepi" Bican was a goalscoring "myth" who was all too real. He is the most prolific goalscorer in football history, with over 805 official goals. He was a player with "2020s" athleticism in the 1930s: he possessed world-class pace (reportedly 10.8s in the 100m), a powerful physique, and was genuinely, completely two-footed, making him a "total" finisher. He was a star of the Austrian "Wunderteam" that reached the 1934 World Cup semi-finals. At club level, he was a god for Slavia Prague, where his goal ratio was an "unbelievable" 1.95 per game (534 goals in 274 matches). WWII and his refusal to join the Nazi party likely robbed him of even greater global fame.
