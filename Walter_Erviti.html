Quick Summary

A highly intelligent and technical left-footed Argentine midfielder, Walter Erviti was a complete professional. He is an eternal legend at Banfield, where he was the captain and star player who led them to their first and only Primera División title (2009 Apertura). He also won multiple titles with San Lorenzo and Boca Juniors in Argentina and a league title with Monterrey in Mexico. He was known for his "privileged" technique, tireless work rate, and on-field intelligence.

Player Overview

Walter Erviti was the quintessential Argentine midfielder: a blend of sublime technique, tactical intelligence, and a warrior's work rate. He began his career at San Lorenzo, winning the 2001 Clausura and Copa Mercosur. He then won a league title in Mexico with Monterrey. However, his legacy was forged at Banfield. As captain and #10, he led the modest club to its first-ever Primera División title in the 2009 Apertura, becoming a god-like figure. He later moved to Boca Juniors, where he was a key piece in winning the 2011 Apertura and reaching the 2012 Copa Libertadores final. He retired as one of the most respected and complete midfielders of his generation.
