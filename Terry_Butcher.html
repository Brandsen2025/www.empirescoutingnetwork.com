Player Overview

Terence Ian Butcher was a "resolute and courageous leader," and the archetypal English centre-half. At 6'4", he was a physically dominant, no-nonsense defender famed for his aerial prowess and fearless tackling. He was a key figure in Sir Bobby Robson's legendary Ipswich Town side, making 271 league appearances and winning the 1981 UEFA Cup. He then moved to Rangers as captain, leading them to three Scottish league titles in four seasons. He captained England and earned 77 caps, but his legacy was immortalized in a 1989 WCQ vs. Sweden, where he played on with his head split open, his white England shirt stained completely red with blood.
