Player Overview

Michael Laudrup, "The Prince," was a player of "pure" footballing intelligence. He was the definitive *fantasista* of his generation, a #10 who played the game with his brain. He was the creative heart of Cruyff's "Dream Team," a side built on technical superiority. Laudrup's game was defined by his "gliding" dribbles and his visionary passing; he could see passes that no one else could, and he famously "invented" the no-look through-ball. He is the only player to win 5 consecutive La Ligas with two different clubs, including a "controversial" move from Barcelona to Real Madrid, whom he immediately led to the title. He was a "gentleman" player, criticized for being "too nice" and not "selfish" enough to score more.
