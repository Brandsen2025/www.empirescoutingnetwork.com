Player Overview

Jim Montgomery is arguably the greatest legend in Sunderland A.F.C. history. A one-club man, he holds the all-time appearance record with 627 matches for the Black Cats. His name is synonymous with the 1973 FA Cup Final, one of the biggest upsets in the competition's history. Playing for Second Division Sunderland against Don Revie's "unbeatable" Leeds United, Montgomery produced a "miraculous" and now-iconic double save—first from Trevor Cherry's header and then somehow, while on the ground, diverting Peter Lorimer's follow-up onto the bar. This save preserved the 1-0 victory and cemented his status as a football immortal.
