Quick Summary

A legendary and tragic hero of Uruguayan football, Roberto Chery was the "consummate goalkeeper" for Peñarol and a Uruguayan champion. Nicknamed "The Poet of the Goal", his career was cut short at age 23. In his one and only match for Uruguay at the 1919 South American Championship, he suffered a strangulated hernia while making a courageous save against Chile. He finished the match but died 13 days later, becoming a martyr for his nation.

Player Overview

Roberto Chery is one of the most poignant figures in football history, a "Poet of the Goal" whose name is synonymous with sacrifice. He was a star goalkeeper for Peñarol, winning the 1918 Uruguayan championship. His elegant, "consummate" style earned him a call-up to the 1919 South American Championship in Brazil. He made his debut on May 17, 1919, against Chile, putting in a "great performance" to secure a 2-0 win. During the match, he made a spectacular save but suffered a strangulated hernia in the process. Refusing to be substituted, he finished the game. He was hospitalized in Rio de Janeiro but died from complications 13 days later, at just 23 years old.
