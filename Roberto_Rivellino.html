Player Overview

Roberto Rivellino (often Rivelino) is one of the greatest attacking midfielders in football history and a symbol of Brazil's "Joga Bonito." He was a superstar of the 1970 World Cup-winning side, playing alongside Pelé, Tostão, and Jairzinho, and scoring 3 goals. He is revered at Corinthians as "Reizinho do Parque" (Little King of the Park), where he played for a decade. He later starred for Fluminense, leading the legendary "Máquina Tricolor" team to two Carioca championships. He was famed for his trademark moustache, his "Elástico" dribble (which he invented), and a ferocious left-footed shot known as the "Patada Atômica" (Atomic Kick).
