Quick Summary

A pacy and skillful Uruguayan forward, Venancio "Chicharra" Ramos was a key figure in one of the most successful eras in South American football. He was an integral part of the legendary Peñarol side that won four Uruguayan titles, the 1982 Copa Libertadores, and the 1982 Intercontinental Cup. He was also a winner on the international stage, claiming the 1980 Mundialito and the 1983 Copa América with Uruguay.

Player Overview

Venancio Ramos is a Uruguayan football legend, a fast and tricky right-winger who defined his era. He began his career at Peñarol in 1977 and became a pillar of their historic "Golden Age," a team that dominated both domestically and continentally. His attacking flair was a key component in their 1982 Copa Libertadores victory, which was immediately followed by winning the Intercontinental Cup against Aston Villa. A star for the national team, he helped Uruguay win the 1980 Mundialito and the 1983 Copa América. He later had a successful spell in France with RC Lens before returning to Uruguay, notably playing for Peñarol's rivals, Nacional.
