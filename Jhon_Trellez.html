Quick Summary

"La Pantera" (The Panther) was a legendary Colombian striker, famed for his power, pace, and lethal finishing. He was a key figure in Atlético Nacional's historic 1989 Copa Libertadores victory. He also starred for Boca Juniors and was a champion and Top Scorer in Switzerland with FC Zürich.

Player Overview

Jhon Jairo "La Pantera" Tréllez was one of the most explosive and powerful strikers in South American history. His nickname perfectly captured his style: he played with a blend of feline agility, raw power (high PHY), and blistering pace (high PAC). He was the star forward for the legendary 1989 Atlético Nacional team that won Colombia's first-ever Copa Libertadores. He also found great success in Europe, winning the Swiss Super League and the Top Scorer award with FC Zürich, and was a fan favorite at Boca Juniors.
