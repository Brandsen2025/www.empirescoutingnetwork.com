Quick Summary

"Der Panzer." The complete and definitive box-to-box midfielder. Famed for his relentless stamina (STA), powerful shooting (SHO), tough tackling (TKL), and generational leadership (CHR). He captained West Germany to 1990 World Cup glory, winning the 1990 Ballon d'Or and 1991 FIFA World Player of the Year. The only outfield player to play in 5 World Cups.

Player Overview

Lothar Matthäus was the ultimate engine, a human "Panzer" tank who combined flawless technical skill with unparalleled physical dominance (STA 10.0). He was the complete midfielder: he could defend (TKL 9.5), pass, dribble, and score spectacular goals from distance (SHO 9.8). He was a generational leader (CHR 10.0), captaining West Germany to 1990 World Cup glory in one of the all-time great individual tournaments. His ability to adapt, later becoming a world-class sweeper, speaks to his generational intelligence (POI 9.7).
