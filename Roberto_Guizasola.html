Quick Summary

Roberto "Cucurucho" Guizasola was a reliable and dynamic Peruvian right-back. A key member of the famous "Category 84" of Alianza Lima (alongside Farfán and Guerrero), he enjoyed a successful domestic career, winning multiple Peruvian league titles. Known for his speed, overlapping runs, and crossing ability, he also had a spell in Argentina with Rosario Central.

Player Overview

Roberto Guizasola was a staple of Peruvian football for over a decade. A product of the Alianza Lima academy, he grew up alongside Jefferson Farfán and Paolo Guerrero. While his friends moved to Europe, Guizasola carved out a fantastic career in South America. He was an attacking right-back, essential for Juan Aurich's historic 2011 title win and Alianza Lima's successes. He briefly played for Rosario Central in Argentina. He is remembered for his relentless running down the right flank and his precise crosses.
