Player Overview

Arsenio Erico is one of the greatest strikers in South American history and the all-time, undisputed top goalscorer of the Argentine Primera División (295 goals in 332 games). A Paraguayan legend, he starred for Independiente in the 1930s and 40s, forming one of the most famous forward lines in history. He was a "virtuoso" known for his prolific scoring, acrobatic finishes, and "prodigious aerial ability," which earned him the nickname "El Saltador Rojo" (The Red Jumper). He won 3 top-scorer titles (1937, 1938, 1939) and 2 league titles with Independiente. He is considered by many, including Alfredo Di Stéfano, to be one of the best ever.
