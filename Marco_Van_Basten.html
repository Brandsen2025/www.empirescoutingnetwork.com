Player Overview

Marco van Basten was the epitome of striking elegance combined with lethal efficiency. His game was defined by unparalleled technique (STK, ELE), allowing him to score spectacular goals (volleys, bicycle kicks, solo runs) with remarkable consistency (GOL, RDS). Possessing excellent height and aerial ability, intelligent movement (POI), and supreme composure (CPX), he was a complete centre-forward. Despite a career cruelly shortened by ankle injuries, his peak performances cemented him as one of the greatest strikers ever.

Strengths and Weaknesses

Strengths

  • Technique & Finishing (STK/GOL/RDS): Generational finisher; sublime technique for volleys, chips, powerful shots.
  • Elegance & Control (ELE/DXT): Graceful movement, exceptional first touch and ball control.
  • Aerial Ability: Excellent heading technique and timing.
  • Composure (CPX): Calm and clinical in front of goal.
  • Football IQ (POI): Intelligent movement and link-up play.

Weaknesses

  • Injuries: Career devastated and ultimately ended by ankle injuries.
  • Pace (SPR): Very good speed, but relied more on technique and timing than pure explosive pace.
  • Physicality (PHY): Less physically imposing than some other elite strikers of the era (e.g., Batistuta).
