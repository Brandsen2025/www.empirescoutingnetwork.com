Quick Summary

Miguel Pardeza was the "forgotten" fifth member of the "Quinta del Buitre." A pacy and intelligent forward, he left Real Madrid early to become an undisputed club legend at Real Zaragoza. He captained the historic Zaragoza team that won the 1994 Copa del Rey and the 1995 UEFA Cup Winners' Cup, scoring in the semi-final.

Player Overview

Miguel Pardeza was the first of the "Quinta del Buitre" to leave Real Madrid, a move that defined his career. Unable to break into the legendary starting XI, he joined Real Zaragoza in 1987 and became their franchise player. A fast, hard-working, and highly intelligent forward, Pardeza was the captain and spiritual leader of the "Euro-Zaragoza" team for over a decade. His greatest triumph came in 1995 when he captained the side to victory in the UEFA Cup Winners' Cup final. He was also a member of Spain's 1990 World Cup squad.
