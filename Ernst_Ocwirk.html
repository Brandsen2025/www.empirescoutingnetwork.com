Player Overview

Ernst "Clockwork" Ocwirk was the "Maestro" of post-war Austrian football. He was a "total" midfielder who started as a striker, but was moved to centre-half, where he revolutionized the position. He was not a "stopper," but a deep-lying "playmaker" (a *Regista*), famed for his "aesthetic" style, technical grace, and pinpoint passing. He was the captain of the 1954 World Cup "Wunderteam" that finished 3rd. He was an icon for Austria Wien (5x league titles) and became a legend at Sampdoria, where he was one of the first Austrians to star in Serie A and served as captain.
