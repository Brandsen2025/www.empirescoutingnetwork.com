Player Overview

Manuel "Manolo" Sanchís Martínez was a Spanish international defender and a key member of the "Yé-yé" Real Madrid side that dominated the 1960s. He was a modern, attacking full-back, known for his relentless pace and work rate. After spells at Condal and Real Valladolid, he joined Real Madrid in 1964. He became a legend, winning four La Liga titles and a Copa del Generalísimo. His most iconic moment came in the 1966 European Cup Final against Partizan, where he scored a crucial long-range goal to help secure a 2-1 victory. He represented Spain at the 1966 World Cup and fathered Manolo Sanchís Hontiyuelo, creating one of football's greatest father-son legacies.
