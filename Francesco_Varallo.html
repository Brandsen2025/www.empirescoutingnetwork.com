Player Overview

Francisco "Pancho" Varallo was one of the first great strikers in professional football and a legend of Boca Juniors. Nicknamed "El Cañoncito" (The Little Cannon) for his incredibly powerful shot, he held the record as Boca Juniors' all-time leading goalscorer for over 70 years, with 194 goals in 222 matches. He was a pure center-forward, combining physical strength with a lethal instinct for goal. He was a key player for Argentina in the inaugural 1930 FIFA World Cup, playing in the final (a loss to Uruguay) as the team's youngest player. With Boca, he was a dominant force, winning three Argentine league titles.
