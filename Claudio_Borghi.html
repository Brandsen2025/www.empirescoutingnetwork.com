Player Overview

Claudio "El Bichi" Borghi is a World Cup-winning Argentine playmaker, considered one of the most talented #10s of his generation. He was the prodigious star of the Argentinos Juniors team that won the 1985 Copa Libertadores, earning him a spot on the 1986 World Cup-winning squad. Borghi was a pure "Enganche," a player who played the game at his own pace, with a "languid" style that masked his genius. He was famed for "la pausa" (the pause), his sublime vision, and his effortless ability to dribble past opponents. His much-hyped move to AC Milan failed, but he remained a cult hero in South America, especially at Colo-Colo.
