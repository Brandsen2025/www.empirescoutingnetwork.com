Quick Summary

The namesake of the legendary "Quinta del Buitre," Emilio Butragueño was one of the most intelligent and clinical strikers of his generation. A "gentleman" forward, he was not fast or strong, but "ghosted" past defenders with sublime dribbling, body feints, and an opportunistic instinct for goal. He led Real Madrid to 6 La Liga titles and 2 UEFA Cups.

Player Overview

Emilio "El Buitre" Butragueño was the symbol of Real Madrid's return to dominance in the 1980s. He was an unconventional striker who relied on pure intelligence. He had a sixth sense for being in the right place, an unpredictable "stop-start" dribbling style that froze defenders, and a clinical, almost surgical, finishing ability. He was the star of the Real Madrid team that won 5 consecutive La Liga titles and 2 UEFA Cups. He famously announced himself to the world by scoring 4 goals against Denmark in the 1986 World Cup.
