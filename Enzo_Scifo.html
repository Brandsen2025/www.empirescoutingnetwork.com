Player Overview

Enzo Scifo was a generational talent, the creative "brain" of the Belgian national team for over a decade. He burst onto the scene at the 1986 World Cup, winning the Best Young Player award and leading Belgium to a historic 4th place finish. His career saw him play for some of Europe's biggest clubs, including Inter Milan and Torino, reaching the UEFA Cup final with the latter. He was a complete playmaker, combining sublime vision, elegant dribbling, and a fine shot. He won 4 Belgian league titles with Anderlecht and a Ligue 1 title with AS Monaco.
