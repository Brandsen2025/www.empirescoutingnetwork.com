Quick Summary

Francisco "Paco" Llorente was a Spanish winger renowned for his blistering, world-class pace. A key member of the legendary "Quinta del Buitre" era at Real Madrid, he was a "super-sub" used to destroy tired defenses. He is the nephew of Paco Gento, brother of Julio Llorente, and father of Marcos Llorente.

Player Overview

Paco Llorente was a classic Spanish winger whose career was defined by one trait: electric speed. He was arguably one of the fastest footballers of his generation. After starting at Atlético Madrid, he made a controversial switch to rivals Real Madrid. There, he became a vital part of the "Quinta del Buitre" team, winning 3 La Liga titles and 2 Copas del Rey. He famously terrorized Porto in a 1987 European Cup match, coming on as a sub to create two goals. He was the perfect "impact sub," a weapon unleashed to exploit pace against fatigued opponents.
