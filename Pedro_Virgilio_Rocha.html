Quick Summary

"El Verdugo," Pedro Virgilio Rocha Franchetti was one of the greatest players of the 1960s/70s, famously called one of the "5 best in the world" by Pelé. He was a god at two clubs: **Peñarol** (winning 3 Copa Libertadores) and **São Paulo** (winning a Série A). He is also the only Uruguayan to play in **four FIFA World Cups** (1962, 1966, 1970, 1974).

Player Overview

Pedro Virgilio Rocha, "El Verdugo" (The Executioner), was a complete attacking force, a player so good that Pelé himself named him one of the five best in the world. He was a classic South American playmaker who was also a prolific goalscorer. He was the star of the Peñarol dynasty in the 1960s, winning eight league titles, three Copa Libertadores, and two Intercontinental Cups. He then moved to Brazil and became a legend for São Paulo, leading them to the 1977 Série A title and finishing as the league's top scorer in 1972. For Uruguay, he was a giant, the only player in the nation's history to feature in four World Cups, and a key member of the 1970 semi-finalist team.
