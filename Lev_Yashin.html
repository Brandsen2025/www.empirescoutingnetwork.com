Player Overview

Lev Ivanovich Yashin is, by consensus, the greatest goalkeeper in the history of football. He revolutionized the position, transforming it from a reactive shot-stopper to a proactive commander (CMD 10.0) and the 11th outfield player. His "Black Spider" nickname stemmed from his iconic all-black kit, imposing stature, and generational athleticism (ATH 9.9), which made his saves (REF 10.0) seem superhuman. He was the first true "sweeper-keeper," dominating his box and starting attacks. His 1963 Ballon d'Or win remains a unique, unrepeated achievement.
