Player Overview

Daley Blind represents the modern evolution of Dutch football intelligence, inheriting his father Danny's exceptional tactical awareness while adding versatility across defensive positions. Unlike his father who was a pure centre-back, Daley excelled across three defensive positions—left-back, centre-back, and defensive midfield—adapting his game to team needs with remarkable consistency. His career highlights include guiding Netherlands to World Cup third place in 2014, winning the Europa League with Manchester United under José Mourinho, and becoming a key figure in Ajax's resurgence in the late 2010s. Currently playing for Girona FC in La Liga, Blind continues to demonstrate that football IQ (POI: 9.1), passing ability (LGP: 9.0), and game-reading skills (INT: 8.6) can overcome physical limitations. While not blessed with exceptional pace or physicality, his intelligence has made him invaluable to every manager he's worked under, from Louis van Gaal to Erik ten Hag.
