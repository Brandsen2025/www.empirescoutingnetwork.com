Player Overview

Eduardo Gonçalves de Andrade, or "Tostão," was one of the most intelligent footballers of his generation. A prolific, left-footed attacking midfielder, he was not a traditional striker but rather a "False 9" who excelled at dropping deep, creating space, and linking play with his sublime vision and technical skill. He was the intellectual force in the attacking "five #10s" of the 1970 Brazil team, forming an unstoppable partnership with Pelé. He is the all-time top scorer for Cruzeiro (249 goals) and was named South American Footballer of the Year in 1971 before an eye injury forced his retirement at only 27.
