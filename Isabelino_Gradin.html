Quick Summary

Uruguay's first great black football star and a legend for both Peñarol and his country. A great-grandson of an enslaved person from Lesotho, Gradin was a true dual-sport athlete, holding South American sprinting records. He translated this "exceptional speed" to the pitch, becoming an unstoppable winger. He won the inaugural 1916 Copa América as the tournament's top goalscorer and won a second in 1917.

Player Overview

Isabelino Gradin is considered one of the most remarkable players of football's early era and Uruguay's first black icon. A great-grandson of African royalty, he was a phenomenal athlete who excelled in two sports. He was a champion South American sprinter, holding records in the 200m and 400m. He famously brought this "exceptional speed" to the football pitch as a right-winger for Peñarol. He was the star of the inaugural 1916 Copa América, leading Uruguay to the title as the tournament's top goalscorer. He won the cup again in 1917 and also led Peñarol to two Uruguayan championships.
