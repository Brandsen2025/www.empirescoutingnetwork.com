Player Overview

Edinson "El Matador" Cavani is one of the most complete and relentless strikers of the 21st century. His game is defined by a supreme work ethic, often pressing defenders like a defensive midfielder, combined with the lethal movement and finishing of an elite poacher. After starring at Palermo, he became a global superstar at Napoli, winning the Serie A top scorer award (29 goals). He then became PSG's all-time leading goalscorer (200 goals), winning 6 Ligue 1 titles. He is Uruguay's second-highest scorer (58 goals) and won the 2011 Copa América.

Strengths and Weaknesses

Strengths

  • Work Rate (WRK): Generational work rate; presses and tracks back relentlessly.
  • Movement (MOV): Elite, intelligent off-ball movement to find space in the box.
  • Finishing (FIN): A "devastating finisher" with both feet and head.
  • Aerial Ability (AER): One of the best headers of the ball in his generation.
  • Versatility: Can play as a lone #9 or on the wing due to his engine.

Weaknesses

  • "Chronic Clumsiness": Infamously missed a high number of "easy" chances.
  • Technical Dribbling: Not an elite 1-v-1 dribbler; relies on movement and power.
  • Injury Prone (Later Career): Suffered muscle injuries, limiting his time at Man Utd and Boca.
