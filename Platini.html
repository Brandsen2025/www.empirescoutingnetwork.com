Player Overview

Michel Platini redefined the attacking midfielder: an elite chance-creator who combined extraordinary vision, a near-perfect set-piece technique and a ruthless scoring instinct from midfield. Dominant across Nancy, Saint-Étienne and Juventus, and captain of France's Euro 1984 winners, Platini's peak output (early-mid 1980s) places him among the highest-impact creators in cross-era comparisons when adjusted for ERA and positional baselines.

Career Milestones & Highlights

1978: Coupe de France winner with Nancy; established national reputation.

1981: Ligue 1 champion with Saint-Étienne.

1983–1985: Three consecutive Ballon d'Ors; capocannoniere Serie A seasons and peak creative output.

1984: Captain and Player of UEFA European Championship; scored 9 goals to lead France to title.

1985: European Cup winner with Juventus (scored penalty in final); Intercontinental Cup success and global recognition.

1986: Key influence in France's run to World Cup semi-final (third place finish).

Post-playing: France coach (1988–1992); co-organiser 1998 World Cup; UEFA president (2007–2015) with later ethics controversy—important for modelling reputational impact in legacy metrics.
