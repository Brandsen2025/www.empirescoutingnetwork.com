Player Overview

Eusébio combined explosive pace, clinical finishing and exceptional athleticism to define the prototype of the modern centre‑forward. He delivered consistent high-volume goal output for Benfica and Portugal across domestic, European and World Cup stages, known for long-distance strikes, powerful volleys and decisive tournament performances.

Career Timeline

Early years in Mozambique; transferred to Benfica in 1960 and immediately integrated into first team; peak period 1962–1968 with consecutive top-scorer seasons and European Cup success; later career includes North American and Mexican spells; long-term ambassadorial role after retirement.
