Quick Summary

A legendary one-club man, Antonio Campolo was the definitive left-winger for Peñarol for his entire 17-year career (1913-1930). He was a serial winner, collecting a staggering *nine* Uruguayan league titles. For the Uruguay national team, he was a key figure in their first era of global dominance, winning the 1924 Olympic Gold Medal in Paris and three Copa América titles (1920, 1924, 1926).

Player Overview

Antonio Campolo is a foundational legend of both Peñarol and the Uruguay national team. A "polifuncional" but legendary left-winger, he was the ultimate one-club man, playing his entire 17-year career for Peñarol. His time at the club was one of unprecedented success, winning nine Uruguayan championships. He was also a core member of Uruguay's first "Golden Generation," the team that traveled to Paris and won the 1924 Olympic Gold Medal, putting South American football on the world map. He also won three Copa América titles, cementing his status as one of the most decorated players of his era.
