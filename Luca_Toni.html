Quick Summary

A 2006 FIFA World Cup winner, Luca Toni was the archetypal Italian "Bomber." A late bloomer who spent years in the lower divisions, he exploded at Palermo before winning the European Golden Shoe with Fiorentina (31 goals). At 1.93m (6'4"), he was a dominant, old-school target man, renowned for his immense strength, aerial prowess, and clinical finishing. He won the Bundesliga with Bayern Munich and, in a remarkable renaissance, became Serie A's oldest-ever Capocannoniere at age 38 with Hellas Verona.

Player Overview

Luca Toni is a 2006 World Cup-winning Italian striker and one of the last great "target-men" of his generation. A classic late bloomer, he toiled in Serie B and C for years before his breakout at Palermo, where he fired them to promotion with 30 goals. He then moved to Fiorentina and produced a legendary 2005-06 season, scoring 31 league goals to win both the Capocannoniere and the European Golden Shoe. This led to a dominant spell at Bayern Munich, where he won a domestic treble. Famed for his 6'4" frame, immense strength, and lethal finishing, he was a nightmare for defenders. Incredibly, he had a career renaissance at Hellas Verona, becoming the oldest player in history (age 38) to win the Capocannoniere in 2014-15.
