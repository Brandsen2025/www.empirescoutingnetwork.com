Quick Summary

A legendary and prolific striker for Barcelona SC and the Ecuador national team, Carlos "El Frentón" Muñoz was a true "Ídolo" of Guayaquil. He was the star forward who led Barcelona to the 1990 Copa Libertadores Final, a 1991 league title, and was the 1992 league top scorer (19 goals). An instinctive poacher, his career was tragically cut short at its peak in a 1993 car accident at age 29, forever cementing his status as a club martyr and legend.

Player Overview

Carlos Antonio Muñoz Martínez was an iconic Ecuadorian centre-forward and a legend of Barcelona Sporting Club. Nicknamed "El Frentón," he was a pure goalscorer, renowned for his poacher's instincts, intelligent movement, and finishing ability. He joined Barcelona in 1990 and immediately became a hero, leading the club to its first-ever Copa Libertadores final that same year. He went on to win the 1991 Ecuadorian championship and was crowned the league's top goalscorer in 1992 with 19 goals. A key player for the Ecuador national team (35 caps, 4 goals), his career was at its absolute peak when he died tragically in a car accident in December 1993 at age 29. He remains an "eterna leyenda" (eternal legend) for Barcelona SC.
