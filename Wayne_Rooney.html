Quick Summary

"Wazza" was a generational English talent, a "street footballer" who combined raw aggression, tireless work rate, and world-class technical skill. He is the all-time leading goalscorer for **Manchester United (253 goals)** and, until 2023, for **England (53 goals)**. A 5x Premier League and 1x Champions League winner, his game was defined by spectacular goals and a ferocious, selfless will to win.

Player Overview

Wayne Rooney was the last of the great "street footballers." He played with a raw, visceral anger that he channeled into breathtaking moments of genius. He was a player of spectacular, "clutch" goals—most famously his 2011 bicycle kick vs. Man City. But his brilliance was in his "total football" DNA. He could play as a #9 (a poacher), a #10 (a creator), or an #8 (a deep-lying passer), and he would do all of them with a ferocious work rate. He sacrificed his own stats to accommodate other superstars (like Ronaldo and Tevez), yet still became the all-time top scorer for both Manchester United (253 goals) and England (53 goals). He was a complete, selfless, and brilliant "warrior."
