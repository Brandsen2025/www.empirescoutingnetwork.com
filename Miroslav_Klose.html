Quick Summary

The all-time leading goalscorer in FIFA World Cup history (16 goals). Miroslav Klose was the ultimate big-tournament striker, a model of consistency, humility, and aerial dominance. While also a prolific Bundesliga scorer, especially at Werder Bremen, his legend was forged with the German national team, culminating in winning the 2014 World Cup. He was renowned for his elite heading ability, poacher's instincts, and famous "Salto" flip celebration.

Player Overview

Miroslav Klose is a German football legend, forever immortalized as the record holder for the most goals scored in FIFA World Cup history (16). While many strikers are defined by their club form, Klose was a phenomenon for the German national team, a reliable and prolific presence across four different World Cups. At club level, he was a powerful and intelligent centre-forward, most notably for Werder Bremen, where he had a sensational 25-goal league season, and for Bayern Munich, where he won two Bundesliga titles. He finished his career at Lazio, becoming a fan favorite. Klose was the complete #9: dominant in the air, a clever poacher, and famously, a model of supreme sportsmanship.
