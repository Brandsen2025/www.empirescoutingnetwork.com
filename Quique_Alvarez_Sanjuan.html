Quick Summary

Son of Quique Costas, Quique Álvarez was an intelligent and technical centre-back who became a legend and captain at Villarreal. A La Masia graduate, he was the leader of the "Yellow Submarine's" defense during their first golden era, culminating in a UEFA Champions League semi-final appearance in 2006. He was known for his elite positioning and leadership.

Player Overview

Quique Álvarez Sanjuán followed his father's footsteps, graduating from La Masia where he was captain of the B team. After spells at Logroñés and Lleida, he joined Villarreal in 1999 and became the club's definitive captain. He was a highly intelligent, modern centre-back who read the game perfectly, compensating for his lack of pace with world-class positioning. He led Villarreal from the Segunda División to a La Liga top-three finish and a historic Champions League semi-final in 2006, playing over 250 games for the club.
