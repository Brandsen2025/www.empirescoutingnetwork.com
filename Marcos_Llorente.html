Quick Summary

A world-class athlete and one of the most versatile players in football, Marcos Llorente is an icon of the Gento/Llorente dynasty. A Real Madrid youth product, he famously transformed from a #6 (Defensive Mid) to a devastating #8/Second Striker under Diego Simeone at Atlético Madrid. His blend of elite stamina, blistering pace, and clinical finishing made him the key player in Atlético's 2020-21 La Liga title win.

Player Overview

Marcos Llorente Moreno is the ultimate modern "hybrid" player, a testament to his elite athletic genes. The son of Paco Llorente and grand-nephew of Paco Gento, he was a tidy defensive midfielder at Real Madrid's academy. His career exploded after a 2019 move to Atlético Madrid. After a famous two-goal performance against Liverpool at Anfield as a forward, Diego Simeone "unlocked" him, transforming him into one of the world's most dangerous box-to-box players. His 2020-21 season (12 goals, 11 assists) was one of the all-time great "hybrid" seasons, leading Atlético to the La Liga title.

Strengths and Weaknesses

Strengths

  • Stamina (STA): "World Class." 10/10. Regarded as a "physical marvel" with a bottomless gas tank.
  • Versatility (VER): "World Class." Has played DM, CM, RM, RWB, RB, and SS at an elite level.
  • Pace (PAC): "Elite." Blistering top-end speed, inherited from his father.
  • Work Rate (WRK): A perfect "Simeone" player. Relentless in his pressing and off-ball runs.
  • Finishing (FIN): Became a clinical, composed finisher during his 2020-21 peak.

Weaknesses

  • "Jack of all Trades": His versatility can sometimes work against him, being used as a "problem-solver" (e.g., at RB) rather than in his best position.
  • Creative Passing: More of a "runner" and "finisher" than a "passer" (like a Modrić or Kroos).
