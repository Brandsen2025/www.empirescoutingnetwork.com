Quick Summary

"El Guajiro" is a legendary Colombian striker, famed for his blistering pace and prolific goal-scoring. He was Colombia's all-time top scorer for over two decades (25 goals in 68 caps) until surpassed by Falcao. He was a key player in the 1990 World Cup, a 1987 Copa América top scorer, and a 2-time Colombian champion with Millonarios.

Player Overview

Arnoldo Alberto Iguarán Zúñiga "El Guajiro" was a complete forward and a national hero for Colombia. His game was defined by truly elite pace (high PAC), which he used to devastating effect on the counter-attack. He was a clinical and prolific finisher (high FIN), scoring 188 career club goals and holding the Colombian national team scoring record (25 goals) for over 20 years. He was the top scorer at the 1987 Copa América and a key member of the 1990 World Cup squad. At club level, he is an all-time idol at Millonarios, where he scored 120 goals and won two league titles (1987, 1988).
