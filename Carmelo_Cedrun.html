Quick Summary

A true legend of Athletic Club, "El Gato" was one of the greatest goalkeepers of his generation. He was the starting keeper for the 1950s "eleven aldeanos" (eleven villagers) dynasty, winning the 1955-56 La Liga title and three Copa del Rey trophies (1955, 1956, 1958). Famed for his agility, reflexes, and security, he won the 1956 Zamora Trophy and represented Spain at the 1962 World Cup. He is the patriarch of a goalkeeping dynasty, father of Andoni Cedrún.

Player Overview

Carmelo Cedrún Ochandátegui is an Athletic Club legend and one of Spain's most celebrated goalkeepers. Nicknamed "El Gato de Amorebieta" for his supreme agility, he was the defensive rock of the iconic 1950s Athletic team that won the 1955-56 La Liga title and a remarkable three Copa del Rey trophies in four years. His brilliance was recognized with the 1955-56 Zamora Trophy for best goalkeeper in the league. He played 404 official matches for Athletic over 14 seasons before finishing his career at RCD Espanyol. He was also Spain's starting goalkeeper at the 1962 FIFA World Cup.
