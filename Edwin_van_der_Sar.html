Quick Summary

One of the greatest goalkeepers in history and the prototype for the modern "Sweeper Keeper". Van der Sar combined immense physical stature with the footwork of an outfield player. He is the only goalkeeper to win the Champions League with two different clubs (Ajax 1995, Man Utd 2008).

Player Overview

Edwin van der Sar revolutionized goalkeeping. His FGA score is anchored by a perfect **10.0 in Distribution (HRG)** and **Composure (CLM)**. He didn't just stop shots; he started attacks. His psychological profile ("The Ice Rabbit") allowed him to perform error-free in high-stakes finals spanning 13 years apart.

Strengths and Weaknesses

Strengths

  • **Distribution (HRG):** Two-footed, capable of 60-yard counter-attacking throws or passes.
  • **Composure (CLM):** Never panicked; radiated calm to his defense.
  • **Positioning (POS):** Rarely needed to make "TV saves" because he was already in position.
  • **Longevity:** Played at world-class level until age 40.

Weaknesses

  • **Low Shots (Early Career):** Occasionally struggled with shots low to his feet due to height (corrected later).
  • **Physical Aggression:** Preferred technique over clearing out bodies in the box.
