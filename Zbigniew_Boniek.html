Player Overview

Zbigniew "Zibi" Boniek was a "total" attacking force and the greatest player in Polish history. He was famed for his "explosive" pace, mazy dribbling, and a "relentless" work rate. His legend was built on "clutch" performances. He led Poland to 3rd place in the 1982 World Cup (scoring a famous hat-trick vs. Belgium) and finished 3rd in the Ballon d'Or that year. At Juventus, he formed a legendary trio with Platini and Rossi. He earned the nickname "Bello di Notte" (Beauty of the Night) from Juventus fans for his "magical" performances in "floodlit" European games, including scoring in the 1G84 Cup Winners' Cup final and winning the 1985 European Cup.
