Player Overview

Néstor "Pipo" Rossi was an Argentine football legend, widely regarded as one of the best "number 5s" of all time. He was the anchor and "voice of the boss" for two of the greatest teams ever assembled: River Plate's "La Máquina" and the Colombian "Ballet Azul" of Millonarios, where he played alongside Alfredo Di Stéfano. Rossi was a "Caudillo"—a field general who commanded the pitch through his grit, flawless positioning, tough tackling, and surprising passing range. He won 9 major league titles and the 1947 Copa América.
