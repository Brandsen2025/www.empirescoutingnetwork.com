Quick Summary

A legendary and highly decorated Uruguayan forward, "El Negro" was one of the most successful players in South American history. A quick, skillful, and combative winger, he was a serial winner. He won 9 Uruguayan league titles, 3 Copa Libertadores (twice with Peñarol, once with Nacional), and 2 Intercontinental Cups. He also starred for Uruguay in 3 World Cups (1962, 1970, 1974).

Player Overview

Luis Alberto "El Negro" Cubilla was a legendary Uruguayan forward and one of the most successful players in the history of the Copa Libertadores. He was a "puntero" (winger) defined by his speed, dribbling, fighting spirit ("garra"), and an incredible knack for winning. He won the Copa Libertadores three times (1960, 1961 with Peñarol; 1971 with Nacional) and the Intercontinental Cup twice. He is one of the few players to be a club legend for both of Uruguay's giants, Peñarol and Nacional. He also starred for Uruguay in three World Cups (1962, 1970, 1974), helping them to a 4th place finish in 1970. He later became a legendary coach, winning two more Libertadores titles.
