Player Overview

Arthur Friedenreich, "El Tigre," was the first great hero of Brazilian football. As a mixed-race player in an era of deep-seated racism, he dominated the amateur game with a style nobody had ever seen. He was a master of the body feint, a dazzling dribbler, and a "mythologically" prolific goalscorer. He was the star of Brazil's Copa América victories in 1919 (where he scored the winning goal in the final) and 1922. While his legendary goal tally of 1,329 is apocryphal, his real impact is not: he was the man who, according to Uruguayan writer Eduardo Galeano, "invented a way of playing." He was the original source code for the "beautiful game."

He *invented* the individual, creative flair that would become Brazil's identity.
