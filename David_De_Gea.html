Player Overview

David De Gea is a Spanish goalkeeper who defined an era at Manchester United. After winning the Europa League with his boyhood club Atlético Madrid, he was signed by Sir Alex Ferguson in 2011. Following a difficult adjustment, he bulked up and evolved into the world's best shot-stopper. His peak from 2014-2018 was a masterclass in reflexes, winning a record four 'Sir Matt Busby Player of the Year' awards as he consistently bailed out underperforming teams. His 14-save performance against Arsenal in 2017 is considered one of the greatest goalkeeping displays in Premier League history. After a year as a free agent, he joined Fiorentina in 2024.

Strengths and Weaknesses

Strengths

  • Reflexes (REF): "Generational." Arguably the best reflex keeper of the 2010s.
  • Shot-Stopping (SHS): "World Class." Elite ability to save with hands and feet.
  • 1-v-1s (ONE): "Very Strong." Spreads himself well and reacts quickly.
  • Durability: Incredibly fit and rarely injured for over a decade.

Weaknesses

  • Distribution (DIS): "Weak." Lacks the passing range of modern "sweeper-keepers."
  • Command of Area: "Average." Reluctant to claim crosses, preferring to stay on his line.
  • Handling: Prone to parrying shots rather than catching them.
