Player Overview

José Marcelo Salas Melinao, "El Matador," was the complete striker. He first conquered Chile with Universidad de Chile before becoming a God at River Plate, where he led them to multiple titles and was named 1997 South American Footballer of the Year. He then starred at the 1998 World Cup, scoring 4 goals (including a brace vs. Italy) and announcing himself to the world. He moved to Lazio, forming a legendary attack and winning a Serie A title and a UEFA Cup Winners' Cup. At his peak, Salas was unplayable: incredibly strong, explosive, dominant in the air, and possessed a cannon of a left foot. His career was unfortunately derailed by injuries, but at his best, he was one of the top 3 strikers in the world.

Strengths and Weaknesses

Strengths

  • Finishing (FIN): Clinical and versatile. Could score with power, finesse, or headers.
  • Shot Power (POW): A devastating left foot.
  • Aerial Ability (AER): Phenomenal leap and heading technique for his size (5' 8").
  • Athleticism (ATH): Explosive, strong, and agile.
  • "Clutch" (MEN): A "big game" player who scored in finals and World Cups.

Weaknesses

  • Injury Prone: A major ACL injury in 2001 effectively ended his "peak" career.
  • Pace (Sustained): Relied on explosive burst rather than pure top-end speed.
  • Discipline: A fiery competitor, could be prone to cards.
