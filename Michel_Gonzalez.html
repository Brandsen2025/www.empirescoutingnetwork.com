Player Overview

José Miguel "Míchel" González is a Spanish football legend, forever immortalized as one of the five members of Real Madrid's "La Quinta del Buitre" (Vulture's Cohort). He was a highly intelligent and technical right-sided midfielder, renowned for his "generational" crossing ability and playmaking vision. He was not a pacy winger, but a "maestro" who dictated the attack from the flank, supplying strikers like Hugo Sánchez and Emilio Butragueño. Over 14 seasons at Real Madrid, he made 559 appearances, winning 6 La Liga titles (including 5 in a row), 2 UEFA Cups, and 2 Copa del Rey. He also scored 21 goals in 66 caps for Spain, starring in the 1990 World Cup.
