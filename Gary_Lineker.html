Quick Summary

One of the greatest goalscorers in English football history. Lineker was an "instinctive forward" renowned for his lethal finishing, intelligent movement, and supreme composure. He won the 1986 World Cup Golden Boot and is famously remembered for never receiving a yellow or red card in his 16-year career.

Player Overview

Gary Lineker was the ultimate "fox-in-the-box," a pure goal-poacher whose game was defined by elite intelligence and economy of motion. He was not a physical or creative player; his genius was in his positioning (high POS) and his "instinctive" ability to be in the right place at the right time. His finishing was clinical and composed (high FIN/CPX), and he possessed a deceptive burst of pace (high SPR). His unique, clean disciplinary record (0 cards) and his 1986 World Cup Golden Boot cement his legacy as a one-of-a-kind #9.

Weaknesses
