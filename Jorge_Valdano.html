Quick Summary

A 1986 World Cup winner with Argentina, Jorge Valdano was the "Philosopher of Football." An elegant, intelligent, and tall centre-forward, he was a key part of Real Madrid's "Quinta del Buitre" era, winning two UEFA Cups and two La Liga titles. He is famously remembered for scoring Argentina's second goal in the 1986 World Cup Final. His cerebral approach to the game defined his post-playing career as a successful manager and influential director.

Player Overview

Jorge Valdano is one of football's great intellectuals, a man who succeeded as a world-class player, a championship-winning manager, and a high-profile club director. As a player, he was an elegant, technical, and highly intelligent striker. After starting at Newell's, he moved to Spain, eventually starring for Real Madrid. There, he formed a famous partnership with Emilio Butragueño and was a core part of the "Quinta del Buitre" team that won back-to-back UEFA Cups and two La Liga titles. His career pinnacle came in 1986, when he played alongside Diego Maradona and scored a crucial goal in the World Cup Final victory against West Germany. He retired early due to illness before becoming a successful coach.
