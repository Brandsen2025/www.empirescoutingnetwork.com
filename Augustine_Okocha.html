Player Overview

Augustine Azuka "Jay-Jay" Okocha was a Nigerian magician, a player who valued joy and entertainment as much as winning. Regarded as one of the best Nigerian and African players ever, he was a quick, talented, and skillful playmaker who played with infectious confidence and flair. He famously scored a 1993 Goal of the Year in Germany, dribbling past multiple players and Oliver Kahn twice. He became the most expensive African player at the time with his move to PSG, where he mentored Ronaldinho. He achieved cult hero status at Bolton Wanderers, bringing unprecedented flair to the Premier League. Internationally, he was a star of Nigeria's "Golden Generation," winning the 1994 AFCON and 1996 Olympic Gold.

Weaknesses
