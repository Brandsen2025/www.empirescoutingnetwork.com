Quick Summary

"The Rocket," Blokhin was one of the most explosive and dominant forwards of his generation. He won the **1975 Ballon d'Or** after leading Dynamo Kyiv to the Cup Winners' Cup. He is the all-time top scorer for both **Dynamo Kyiv (266 goals)** and the **Soviet Union (42 goals)**, famed for his blistering pace, power, and lethal finishing.

Player Overview

Oleg Blokhin, "The Tsar," was a force of nature. He was a forward who played with the explosive speed of a 100-meter sprinter, which earned him his other nickname, "The Rocket." For nearly two decades, he was the superstar of Dynamo Kyiv and the Soviet Union. His game was built on raw, blistering pace, which he used to tear past defenders before unleashing a powerful and accurate shot. His 1975 season is one of the greatest in history: he led Dynamo Kyiv to the Cup Winners' Cup (scoring in the final) and the UEFA Super Cup (scoring all 3 goals vs. Bayern Munich), earning him the Ballon d'Or over legends like Beckenbauer and Cruyff. He is the all-time top scorer for the Soviet League, Dynamo Kyiv, and the USSR national team.
