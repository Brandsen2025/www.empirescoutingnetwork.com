Player Overview

Ricardo "El Beto" Infante was an Argentine football legend. An Estudiantes icon, he played 18 seasons for the club, becoming their all-time top scorer (180 goals) and the 6th highest scorer in Argentine league history (217 goals). He was a complete forward, known for his clinical finishing, powerful aerial game, and surprising technical skill. His most famous moment came on September 19, 1948, when he scored an incredible 35-meter rabona goal against Rosario Central, an act of improvisation that has become football folklore and one of the earliest documented uses of the technique.
