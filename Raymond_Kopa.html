Player Overview

Raymond Kopa (born Kopaszewski) was a footballing pioneer. He was a diminutive (1.69m) attacking midfielder whose game was built on "fantastic" close-quarters dribbling (DXT 10.0) and "great intelligence" (POI 10.0). A renowned playmaker and prolific scorer, he was the creative force for both the legendary Reims and Real Madrid teams, forming a "fearsome" forward line with Di Stéfano, Puskás, and Gento. His 1958 Ballon d'Or win cemented his status as the world's best player.
