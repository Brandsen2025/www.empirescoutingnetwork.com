Player Overview

Willington Ortiz is a Colombian football icon, frequently named as the country's greatest player of the 20th century. "El Viejo Willy" was a forward who played with explosive pace, agility, and "superb" dribbling ability, terrorizing defenses in the Colombian league for nearly two decades. He was a serial champion, winning two league titles with Millonarios before moving to Deportivo Cali. He then became the star of the legendary América de Cali team that won four consecutive league titles (1983, 1984, 1985, 1986) and reached three consecutive Copa Libertadores finals (1985, 1986, 1987). For Colombia, he earned 49 caps and played in 3 Copa Américas, leading them to the 1975 final.
