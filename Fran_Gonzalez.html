Player Overview

Francisco "Fran" González is a Spanish football legend and the ultimate "one-club man," having spent his entire 17-year career at Deportivo La Coruña. He was the captain and creative soul of the "Super Depor" team, the greatest side in the club's history. An elegant, left-footed midfielder with "generational" vision and technique, "O Neno" (The Kid) was the team's leader. He famously rejected offers from Real Madrid and Barcelona to stay with his club, leading them to a historic La Liga title in 1999–2000, two Copa del Rey titles (1995, 2002), and three Spanish Super Cups. He is the father of FC Porto midfielder Nico González.
