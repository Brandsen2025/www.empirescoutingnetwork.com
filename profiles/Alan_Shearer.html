Quick Summary

Alan Shearer is the Premier League's all-time top scorer with 260 goals—a record that stands untouched. A complete striker combining power, finishing, heading ability, and leadership, he was the prototype modern number 9. His £15 million move to hometown club Newcastle United in 1996 turned down Manchester United to become a Geordie legend, scoring 206 goals across 10 seasons despite serious injuries.

Player Overview

Alan Shearer represents everything a number 9 should be: clinical finishing, dominant aerial presence, physical strength, intelligent movement, leadership, and unwavering dedication. Standing 6'0" with a powerful build, he bullied defenders while possessing the technical finesse to score spectacular goals. His heading ability was virtually unmatched—perfectly timed runs, tremendous leap, and unerring accuracy made him devastating from crosses. Despite suffering three serious knee injuries (two ACL ruptures), he remarkably maintained elite performance throughout his career. His loyalty to Newcastle United, turning down Manchester United at his peak, cemented his status as a Geordie icon and one of English football's most beloved figures.
