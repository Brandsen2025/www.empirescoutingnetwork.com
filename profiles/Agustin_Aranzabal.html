Player Overview

Agustín Aranzábal is a Spanish football legend and an icon of Real Sociedad. As the son of club "GOAT" Gaztelu, he carried on the family dynasty, becoming a "one-club man" for 13 seasons and making over 350 appearances. He was an "elite" modern left-back, known for his "generational" stamina, relentless work rate, and attacking pace. He was the starting left-back for the legendary 2002-03 Real Sociedad team, alongside Xabi Alonso, who shocked Spain and finished runners-up in La Liga. He was also a 28-cap Spain international, representing his country at the 1998 World Cup and Euro 2000.
