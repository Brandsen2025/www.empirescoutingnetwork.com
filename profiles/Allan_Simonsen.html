Quick Summary

"The Flea," Simonsen was a "tiny giant" (1.65m) and the only Danish player to ever win the **1977 Ballon d'Or**. He was a "clutch" player, famed for his blistering pace, low center of gravity, and powerful shot. He is the only player to score in all three major European finals (European Cup, CWC, and UEFA Cup).

Player Overview

Allan Simonsen was a "superstar" packed into a 1.65m (5' 5") frame. He was a dynamic, fast, and incredibly agile forward. He was a key part of the legendary Borussia Mönchengladbach team of the 1970s, winning 3 Bundesliga titles and 2 UEFA Cups (scoring in the 1975 & 1979 finals). His "clutch" play was his trademark; he also scored in the 1977 European Cup final (vs. Liverpool) and the 1982 Cup Winners' Cup final (for Barcelona). This "3-final" record, combined with his team's success, led him to win the 1977 Ballon d'Or, beating Kevin Keegan and Michel Platini.
