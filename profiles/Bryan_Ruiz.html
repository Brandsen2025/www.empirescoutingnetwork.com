Player Overview

Bryan Ruiz was the quintessential "luxury" playmaker, defined by his exceptional technical quality (DXT), vision (KEY), and elegance (ELE). His game was based on intelligence (POI) and creativity (CRK, FLA), not physical attributes. He possessed a sublime left foot, capable of scoring spectacular finesse goals (STK) and delivering defense-splitting passes (LGP). While his lack of pace (SPR) and low defensive work rate (TPI) were notable, he was the creative fulcrum for Eredivisie-winning FC Twente and the captain who led Costa Rica on their historic 2014 World Cup run.

Strengths and Weaknesses

Strengths

  • Technique & Elegance (DXT/ELE): Sublime first touch, balance, and graceful movement.
  • Composure (CPX): Extremely calm on the ball; excellent decision-maker.
  • Finishing (Finesse - STK): Scored many goals with precise, curled shots.
  • Leadership (CHR): Inspirational captain for Costa Rica.

Weaknesses

  • Pace & Athleticism (SPR/PHY): Very slow; one of the least athletic top-tier players.
  • Defensive Work Rate (TPI/MRK): Minimal defensive contribution or pressing.
  • Physicality: Very lightweight; easily pushed off the ball.
  • Consistency (CON): Prone to drifting out of games if not on the ball.
