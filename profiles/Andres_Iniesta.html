Player Overview

Andrés Iniesta Luján was a footballing artist, the master of space and time. Alongside Xavi, he was the engine of "Tiki-Taka," but where Xavi was the metronome, Iniesta was the catalyst. His game was defined by generational press-resistance (TEC 10.0) and his signature "La Croqueta" dribble (DXT 10.0), allowing him to glide through impossible spaces. He combined this with flawless decision-making (DMA 10.0) and elite creativity (KEY 9.8). He is, perhaps, the greatest "big-game" midfielder ever, scoring the 2010 World Cup Final winner and assisting in the 2009 CL Semi-Final.
