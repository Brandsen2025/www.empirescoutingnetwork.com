Player Overview

Abedi Ayew, known globally as Abedi Pele, is a Ghanaian legend and one of the first African players to become a true superstar in Europe. An attacking midfielder with hypnotic dribbling skills, sublime vision, and a penchant for game-winning plays, he was given the nickname "Pelé" in recognition of his superior ability. His fame peaked at Marseille, where he was part of the "Magical Trio" with Papin and Waddle. He led Marseille to two European Cup finals, culminating in their historic 1993 Champions League victory over AC Milan. He won the African Player of the Year award three straight times (1991-93) and an AFCON title with Ghana in 1982.
