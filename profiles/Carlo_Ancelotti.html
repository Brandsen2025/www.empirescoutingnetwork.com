Before becoming the most successful Champions League manager in history, Carlo Ancelotti was an elegant central midfielder who won it all as a player. Born June 10, 1959 in Reggiolo, Italy, the 1.79m midfielder enjoyed remarkable success with AS Roma and AC Milan. **AS Roma (1979-1987)**: **171 appearances, 12 goals**. Eight seasons as key midfielder, becoming captain. Won **Serie A 1982-83** and **4x Coppa Italia** (1980, 1981, 1984, 1986). Despite knee injuries that cost significant playing time, including missing 1984 European Cup final, remained pivotal figure for the Giallorossi. **AC Milan (1987-1992)**: **112 appearances, 10 goals**. Integral part of Arrigo Sacchi's legendary squad that dominated European football. As central midfielder, contributed to **2x Serie A** (1988), **2x European Cups** (1989, 1990), **2x European Super Cups**, **2x Intercontinental Cups**, and **Supercoppa Italiana**. Memorable long-range goal vs Real Madrid in victorious European Cup campaign. Played alongside Gullit, Rijkaard, Van Basten, and young Maldini in one of greatest teams ever assembled. **Italy (26 caps)**: International career representing Azzurri. **Retired age 33** due to persistent knee problems. Known for elegant technique, exceptional passing, ability to break up play and initiate attacks. Transitioned seamlessly from legendary player to legendary manager - rare double success.
