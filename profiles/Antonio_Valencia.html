Quick Summary

One of the greatest Ecuadorian players ever, "El Tren" was a model of consistency, power, and professionalism for Manchester United for a decade. Signed as a pacy winger, he successfully converted into one of the world's best right-backs, winning the Sir Matt Busby Player of the Year in 2011-12 and captaining the side to a Europa League title.

Player Overview

Antonio Valencia's career is a testament to professionalism and adaptation. He arrived at Manchester United from Wigan in 2009 as a raw, powerful right-winger, earning a spot in the PFA Team of the Year in his debut season. His 2011-12 campaign was his individual peak as a winger, winning him the Sir Matt Busby Player of the Year. He later successfully transitioned into a "strong, quick and athletic right back," becoming a model of consistency and a leader in the dressing room. He eventually earned the club captaincy and lifted the 2017 UEFA Europa League trophy. Over 10 years at United, he made 339 appearances and won 2 Premier League titles, 1 FA Cup, and 2 League Cups.

  • Work Rate (DYN): A tireless engine, perfect for a two-way winger or modern fullback.
  • Crossing (CRO): A powerful and consistent crosser of the ball, especially from the byline.
  • Professionalism & Versatility: A model professional who successfully transitioned from elite winger to elite fullback.

Weaknesses

  • One-Footed: Overwhelmingly reliant on his right foot.

  • Goal Scoring: Not a natural finisher; his goal tally was modest.
