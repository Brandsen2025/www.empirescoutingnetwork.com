Player Overview

Alex Cropley was a Scottish international midfielder who represented the "artist" class of the 1970s. He was a pivotal player for the legendary Hibernian side known as "Turnbull's Tornadoes," a team famed for its attacking flair. Cropley's elegance, vision, and "exceptional" creative skill were central to their 1972 League Cup victory. His talent earned him a major transfer to Arsenal in 1974, and he later became a star at Aston Villa, winning another League Cup in 1977. His immense potential was ultimately unfulfilled, as his career was cruelly derailed by two separate compound leg fractures, the first at Arsenal and the second at Villa.
