Player Overview

Ángel Amadeo Labruna, "El Feo," is perhaps the single most important figure in the history of River Plate. He was the goalscoring heart of "La Máquina," the legendary 1940s team that revolutionized football with its "1-2-3-4-5" passing and movement. Labruna was the inside-left, a prolific, intelligent, and fiercely competitive forward. His 294 goals in the Primera División is a record that has stood for over 60 years. He was also a symbol of *El Superclásico*, and is the all-time top scorer in the derby against Boca Juniors (16 goals). After his 20-year, 9-title playing career, he returned as a manager to break River's 18-year title drought, cementing his status as the club's ultimate idol.
