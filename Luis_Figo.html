Player Overview

Luís Figo was one of the defining players of his generation, a genius with the ball at his feet. He was a classic winger who could operate on either flank, specializing in humiliating defenders with his dribbling before delivering a perfect cross. More than just a winger, he was a creative playmaker and a leader, captaining Portugal for years. His controversial 2000 transfer from Barcelona to Real Madrid broke the world record and ignited the "Galácticos" era. He won the Ballon d'Or (2000), FIFA World Player of the Year (2001), and the Champions League (2002).
