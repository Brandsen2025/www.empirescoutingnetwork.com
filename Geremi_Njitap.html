Player Overview

Geremi Sorele Njitap Fotso is a Cameroonian legend and one of the most decorated players in African football history. He was a powerful, energetic, and tactically brilliant utility player, capable of excelling as a right-back, wing-back, defensive midfielder, or central midfielder. His tireless work rate and intelligence made him an invaluable squad member for two of the biggest clubs in the world. He won two Champions League titles (2000, 2002) and one La Liga title with Real Madrid's "Galácticos." He then became a key player for José Mourinho's Chelsea, winning two consecutive Premier League titles (2005, 2006). Internationally, he was a giant, winning Olympic Gold in 2000 and two Africa Cup of Nations titles (2000, 2002).
