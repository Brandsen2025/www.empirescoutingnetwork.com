Player Overview

Pedro "El Vasco" Cea was the "clutch" goalscorer for the greatest dynasty in football history. While Scarone was the "magician" and Andrade the "pillar," Cea was the "executioner." An intelligent and prolific inside-left, he had an uncanny knack for scoring the most important goals. He was a 2x Olympic Gold medalist and 2x Copa América winner. His legend was cemented in the 1930 World Cup, where he was the tournament's second-highest scorer (5 goals) and, most importantly, scored the 57th-minute equalizing goal in the final against Argentina, turning the tide for Uruguay. He is an icon for both Nacional and Bella Vista.
