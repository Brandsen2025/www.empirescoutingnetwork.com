Player Overview

Tomás Felipe "El Trinche" Carlovich is less a player and more a folk hero. He is the "invisible genius" of football, a player widely considered by those who saw him (including Maradona and Menotti) to be one of the greatest of all time. He played most of his career in the lower divisions of Argentina for Central Córdoba, rejecting fame and fortune. His style was described as "lyrical" and "elegant." He was a tall, unhurried #5 who controlled the game with impossible dribbling (famously inventing the "double nutmeg") and perfect vision. His legend was cemented in a 1974 friendly, where a Rosario XI (starring Carlovich) was beating the Argentine national team 3-0 by halftime. The national coach reportedly begged for "that #5" (Carlovich) to be subbed off, as he was humiliating the national side.

Philosophy & Experience

Carlovich is a psychological case study. He was a footballing genius who was completely detached from the professional world. He was the "Syd Barrett" of football. His motivation was purely intrinsic: he played to play. He famously said he preferred to play on his local pitch, for low stakes, with his friends. This "anti-fame" mindset is what makes his legend so powerful and "romantic."
