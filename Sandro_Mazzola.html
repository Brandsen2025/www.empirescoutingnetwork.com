Player Overview

Sandro Mazzola was the "modern" half of Italy's most famous football debate. While his rival Gianni Rivera was a slow, cerebral "artist," Mazzola was a dynamic, explosive, and hard-working attacker. A "one-club" legend for Inter, he was the offensive catalyst for Helenio Herrera's "La Grande Inter" dynasty. He was a versatile forward who could play as a #10, a second striker, or a winger, using his incredible pace and dribbling to launch devastating counter-attacks. He scored two goals in the 1964 European Cup final, won the 1968 EUROs, and finished 2nd in the 1971 Ballon d'Or. His rivalry with Rivera was so intense, the national team invented the "Staffetta" (Relay), where they would each play one half of a match.
