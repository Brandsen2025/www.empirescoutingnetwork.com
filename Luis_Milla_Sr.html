Quick Summary

Luis Milla was a highly intelligent and technical defensive midfielder, one of the few players to successfully play for both FC Barcelona and Real Madrid. A product of La Masia, he was a key part of Johan Cruyff's "Dream Team" before his controversial move to Madrid, where he won two La Liga titles. He finished his career at Valencia, winning a final Copa del Rey.

Player Overview

Luis Milla Aspas was an intelligent and elegant Spanish midfielder who defined the "pivot" role in the late 80s and 90s. He emerged from FC Barcelona's La Masia as a key talent for Johan Cruyff, winning La Liga and a Cup Winners' Cup. In 1990, he made a high-profile, controversial transfer to arch-rivals Real Madrid. There, he became a reliable anchor, adding two more La Liga titles and a Copa del Rey to his collection. He finished his decorated career at Valencia, winning one last Copa del Rey. He was known for his elite positioning, calm distribution, and technical security.
