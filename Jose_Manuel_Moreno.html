Player Overview

José Manuel "El Charro" Moreno was an Argentine icon and a "precursor to the 'total footballer' concept." He was the undisputed star of River Plate's legendary "La Máquina" (The Machine) forward line, a team that dominated the 1940s. Moreno was a "complete player," blending "great technique," "excellent vision," "formidable heading ability," and "lethal" finishing (179 goals in 321 games for River). He was the first player in history to win league titles in four different countries (Argentina, Mexico, Chile, Colombia). With Argentina, he won three Copa Américas, and was named the tournament's Best Player in 1947.
