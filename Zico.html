Player Overview

Arthur Antunes Coimbra, "Zico," was the definitive Brazilian "No. 10" of his era, the "White Pelé." His game was pure artistry, defined by generational intelligence (POI 10.0), vision, and creativity (KEY 10.0). He was a prolific goalscorer (GOL 9.8) and arguably the greatest free-kick taker in history. He was the undisputed leader of Flamengo's golden era, famously dismantling Liverpool in the 1981 Intercontinental Cup, and the creative hub of the magical 1982 Brazil World Cup squad.
