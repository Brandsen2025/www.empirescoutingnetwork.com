Player Overview

Giuseppe "Peppino" Meazza was, by all accounts, the greatest player of the pre-war era alongside Scarone and Sindelar. He was a "total" forward who could do everything: he was a prolific goalscorer (3x Serie A top scorer), a visionary passer, and a "dribbling-maestro." He led Inter (then Ambrosiana) to 3 Serie A titles and is their all-time top scorer (284 goals). His international career is legendary: he was the star of the 1934 World Cup-winning team and the *captain* of the 1938 World Cup-winning team. He is one of only three Italians (with Ferrari and Monzeglio) to win both. His legacy is so immense that the stadium for both Inter and AC Milan is named "Stadio Giuseppe Meazza."
