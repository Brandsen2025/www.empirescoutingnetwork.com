Player Overview

Raimundo "Mumo" Orsi was a "double-champion" of the pre-war era. A small, lightning-fast, and tricky left-winger, he was a superstar in both Argentina and Italy. He first starred for Argentina, winning the 1927 Copa América and a Silver Medal at the 1928 Olympics. His success earned him a move to Juventus, where he became a key part of the "Quinquennio d'Oro" (Golden Quinquennium) team that won 5 straight league titles. As an "oriundo," he switched allegiance to Italy and became a national hero, scoring the crucial 81st-minute equalizer in the 1934 World Cup final, which Italy went on to win.
