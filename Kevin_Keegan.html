Quick Summary

"King Kev," Keegan was one of the first "superstar" English players. He was a dynamic, relentless, and prolific forward. He won the **Ballon d'Or** in back-to-back years (**1978 & 1979**) while at Hamburger SV. Before that, he was the star of the 1970s Liverpool dynasty, winning 3x League titles and the **1977 European Cup**.

Player Overview

Kevin Keegan, "King Kev," was an icon of 1970s football. He was a "pocket-rocket" forward who combined a "superstar" persona with a "working-class" work ethic. He was relentless, a player of perpetual motion who pressed, tackled, and ran, all while being a world-class dribbler and finisher. He was the star #7 for the Liverpool dynasty, forming a legendary partnership with John Toshack and winning the 1977 European Cup. He then shocked the world by moving to Hamburg, where he elevated the club, won the 1978-79 Bundesliga, and won two consecutive Ballon d'Or awards (1978, 1979).
