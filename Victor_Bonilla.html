Quick Summary

A powerful and prolific Colombian striker, Víctor Bonilla was a dominant goal-scorer, especially for Deportivo Cali, where he became a club legend. He was the 1999 Copa Libertadores Top Scorer, leading Cali to the final, and was a key forward for the Colombian national team, featuring in the 1999 Copa América and 2000 CONCACAF Gold Cup.

Player Overview

Víctor Manuel Bonilla Hinestroza was a classic South American No. 9, blending power, sharp movement, and lethal finishing. His peak came at Deportivo Cali, where he formed a famed partnership and led the team to a league title. His exploits in the 1999 Copa Libertadores, where he won the Golden Boot, earned him a move to Europe. He was a complete forward for his time, dangerous on the ground (high FIN) and in the air (high AER), with a powerful shot (high POW).
