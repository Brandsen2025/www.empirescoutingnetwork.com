Quick Summary

Anderson Luís de Souza, "Deco," was the ultimate "industrious artist." He is one of the very few players to win the UEFA Champions League with two different clubs (Porto, 2004; Barcelona, 2006). A Brazilian-born Portuguese international, he was the creative brain of José Mourinho's Porto and a key link in Frank Rijkaard's Barcelona, blending sublime vision, technique, and creative passing with a relentless, gritty work rate.

Player Overview

Deco is a Portuguese football legend who defined the "complete" midfielder. He was not just a luxury playmaker; he was a tactical brain with the engine of a workhorse. He rose to global fame as the leader of José Mourinho's 2004 Champions League-winning Porto side, earning the UEFA Club Footballer of the Year award. He then moved to Barcelona, where he formed a new midfield dynasty alongside Xavi and Iniesta, winning another Champions League in 2006. His ability to blend "Menotti Artistry" (creativity, vision) with "Simeone Cholismo" (work rate, tackling) made him an indispensable component of every team he was on, including Chelsea, where he won a Premier League title.

Weaknesses
