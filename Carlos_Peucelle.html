Quick Summary

Carlos Peucelle, known as "Barullo" and "El Primer Millonario" (The First Millionaire), was Argentina's first superstar footballer and one of the country's greatest wingers. He was a key member of Argentina's 1930 World Cup runner-up squad and River Plate's legendary teams of the 1930s. His 10,000-peso transfer to River Plate in 1931 made him the first "millionaire" player in Argentine football history, revolutionizing the professional game.

Player Overview

Carlos Desiderio Peucelle was a revolutionary figure in Argentine football, both as a player and as a symbol of the sport's professionalization. As a left winger and inside forward, he possessed exceptional dribbling ability, precise crossing, and clinical finishing—a complete attacking threat. His speed and technical brilliance made him one of the most feared attackers of the 1930s. Beyond his on-field excellence, his landmark 10,000-peso transfer from Sportivo Buenos Aires to River Plate in 1931 transformed Argentine football economics, earning him the nickname "El Primer Millonario" and paving the way for the modern professional game. He won four league titles with River Plate and represented Argentina at the first-ever World Cup in Uruguay (1930), where La Albiceleste finished runners-up.
