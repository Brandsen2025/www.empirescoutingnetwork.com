Player Overview

Dany Mota's FGA rating is largely driven by his **Work Rate (TPI)** and his effectiveness during transitional moments (**OMS**). He is not primarily an elite technical player, but his tactical discipline and tireless running make him a valuable asset for systems that prioritize high-line pressing and quick ball recovery (Klopp/Palladino philosophy). His physicality is well-suited for the demanding nature of Serie A.

Strengths and Weaknesses

Strengths

  • Pressing (TPI/PRE): Tireless, tactically intelligent presser of the back line.
  • Transition Threat (OMS): Dangerous on the counter; high acceleration over short distances.
  • Finishing: Reliable goalscorer (GOL) from inside the box.
  • Versatility (INT): Equally effective at CF or LW/RW.

Weaknesses

  • Dribbling (DXT): Not an elite 1v1 creator; relies on speed and movement.
  • Passing Range: Primarily a functional passer, not a deep-lying playmaker.
