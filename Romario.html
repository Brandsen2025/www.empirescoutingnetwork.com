Player Overview

Romário de Souza Faria, known as Romário, was a striker who defined "lethal." He was a penalty-box genius, combining generational intelligence (POI 10.0) with an explosive burst of pace (ACC 9.9) and the most clinical, varied finishing (FIN 10.0) imaginable. He was the master of the "toe-poke" and 1-v-1 situations (CMP 9.9). Johan Cruyff called him a "genius" of the penalty area, and his 1994 World Cup performance, where he dragged Brazil to victory, is one of the most dominant by a single player in tournament history.
