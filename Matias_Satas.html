Quick Summary

A highly-touted centre-back from the Boca Juniors academy, Satas is the captain of the Argentina Under-17 team. He is the first player born in 2008 to sign a professional contract with Boca. A strong, left-footed defender, he is praised for his leadership, hard-working attitude, and embodying the "typical Boca defender" mentality.

Player Overview

Matías Satas is one of the premier defensive prospects in South America. A product of the "Boca Predio" since age five, he embodies the club's famous defensive grit. His coach, Antonio Barijho, describes him as a "typical Boca defender" who "fights for every ball like it is the last one in his life." This mentality, combined with a strong 1.85m frame, a good left foot, and natural leadership (captain of Argentina U-17s), led him to become the first player born in 2008 to sign a professional contract with the club. He is a high-ceiling prospect with the technical and mental tools to succeed at the highest level.

Psychological Profile & Personality Analysis

Satas is noted for his exemplary attitude. With a policeman father and teacher mother, he is grounded and hard-working. His status as U-17 captain confirms his natural leadership and maturity. He is described as respectful and driven, a mentality that is essential for surviving the "Boca Predio" and making the leap to the first team. He has the ideal psychological profile for a top-tier defender.

Philosophy & Experience

Satas is a pure product of the Boca Juniors defensive philosophy, which demands a non-negotiable level of aggression, commitment, and toughness. His experience as captain of the Argentina U-17s has supplemented this with tactical and leadership experience at the international level, making him one of the most well-rounded defensive prospects in his age group.
