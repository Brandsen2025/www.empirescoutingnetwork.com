Player Overview

Omar Sívori, "El Cabezón," was a genius and a rebel. He was one of the "Angels with Dirty Faces" (Los Carasucias), the legendary Argentine front three that won the 1957 Copa América. He then moved to Juventus for a world-record fee, where he became an icon, winning the 1961 Ballon d'Or as an *oriundo* (playing for Italy). A left-footed "trequartista," he played with his socks rolled down, inviting defenders to kick him, only to humiliate them with his supreme dribbling and agility. He formed the "Holy Trinity" at Juve with John Charles and Giampiero Boniperti. His game was a mix of sublime art and pure provocation.
