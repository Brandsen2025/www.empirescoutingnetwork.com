Quick bio
"Rocky" Rocastle was an Arsenal academy product who became a beloved attacking midfielder and winger, driving George Graham's mid-1980s resurgence and later contributing across several Premier League clubs. Renowned for skill, work-rate and grace under pressure, Rocastle remained a fan icon long after his passing.

Player Overview

Profile: David Carlyle Rocastle (2 May 1967 – 31 Mar 2001) combined technical grace, close control and intelligent movement with a work-rate uncommon in classical number 10s. He functioned as both playmaker and wide creator, thriving between the lines and on the flanks.

Style: Able to operate centrally or on the right, Rocastle blended skillful ball progression with timely runs and defensive contribution—bridging artistry and team duty.

Role fit: Suited to 4-4-2 (traditional wing/AM hybrid), 4-2-3-1 as the right-sided 10, or a supporting wide playmaker role that demands both creativity and transitional work.
