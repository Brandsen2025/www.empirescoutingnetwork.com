Player Overview

Ademir Marques Menezes, "Queixada" (The Jaw), was Brazil's first truly "modern" and devastating centre-forward. While Leônidas was an acrobat, Ademir was a bullet. He possessed a rare combination of explosive pace, a powerful shot with both feet, and intelligent movement that tore defenses apart. He was the star of the 1949 Copa América (Best Player, Top Scorer) and the 1950 World Cup, where he won the Golden Boot. Though that tournament ended in tragedy (the *Maracanazo*), Ademir's individual brilliance was undisputed. At club level, he was the icon of Vasco da Gama's "Expresso da Vitória," one of the greatest club teams of the era.
