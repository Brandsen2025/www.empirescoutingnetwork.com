Quick Summary

"El Tigre" was a tall, prolific, and complete Argentine forward, renowned for his goal-scoring instincts and ability to link up play. A legend at América de Cali, he led them to two Colombian titles and three consecutive Copa Libertadores finals. He was also a top scorer for Boca Juniors.

Player Overview

As a player, Ricardo "El Tigre" Gareca was a dominant and prolific centre-forward. Standing at 1.86m (6 ft 1 in), he was a strong physical presence (high PHY) and a major aerial threat (high AER). His game was built on lethal finishing (high FIN), scoring over 200 professional goals. He was not just a poacher; he possessed intelligent movement and good technique (high MOV, high TEC), allowing him to link up play effectively (high LKP). His legendary status was forged at América de Cali, where he was the star striker, winning two league titles and becoming the 1987 Copa Libertadores top scorer.

Strengths and Weaknesses

Strengths

  • Goal Poacher (FIN): Elite finishing instincts inside the box; a prolific scorer with over 200 career goals.
  • Aerial Dominance (AER): At 1.86m, he was a primary target on crosses and set pieces.
  • Physicality (PHY): Strong centre-forward who could hold up the ball and battle defenders.
  • Big Game Player: Scored in crucial title races and international qualifiers.
  • Link-up Play (LKP): More than just a target man, he could bring supporting attackers into the game.

Weaknesses

  • Pace: Not known for explosive speed; relied on positioning and strength.
  • Copa Libertadores "Curse": Famously part of the América de Cali team that lost three straight finals.
  • Controversial Transfers: His direct move from Boca to River Plate made him an enemy of many Boca fans.
