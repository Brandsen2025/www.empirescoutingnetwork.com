Quick Summary

The younger brother of Kolo and Yaya, Ibrahim Touré was a professional footballer who also played as a striker. While he did not reach the global heights of his brothers, he forged a solid professional career, playing for ASEC Mimosas in his home country, Metalurh Donetsk in Ukraine, OGC Nice in France's Ligue 1, and several clubs in the Middle East. He tragically passed away at the age of 28.

Player Overview

Ibrahim Touré was the third member of the famous Ivorian footballing family. A tall and powerful centre-forward, he shared his family's athletic gifts. He began his career at ASEC Mimosas and Metalurh Donetsk before earning a move to France with OGC Nice. He later had a very prolific spell in the Egyptian Premier League and for Lebanese club Al-Safa, where he was a key player. Tragically, his career was cut short when he passed away in 2014 at age 28 after a short illness, while his brothers were competing at the World Cup.
