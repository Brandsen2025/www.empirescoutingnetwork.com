Player Overview

Paul "Polle Gazon" van Himst is, simply, Belgian football royalty. Named his nation's "Golden Player" by UEFA, he was the "Belgian Pelé" of his generation. He was a "complete" forward, blending the creativity of a #10 with the finishing of a #9. He was a sublime dribbler (earning the nickname "Polle Gazon" or "Polle Lawn" because he was fouled so often), a visionary passer, and a prolific goalscorer (233 goals for Anderlecht). He was a "one-club" man, leading Anderlecht to 8 league titles and winning the Belgian Golden Shoe a record 4 times. For Belgium, he was the star of the 1970 World Cup and the 1972 EUROs (3rd place).
