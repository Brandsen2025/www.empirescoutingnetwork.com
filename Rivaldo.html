Player Overview

Rivaldo was a generational attacking talent, a match-winner defined by his sublime left foot, individual genius, and flair. His profile is characterized by elite technical skill (DXT, STK, FLA), allowing him to score some of the most spectacular goals in history (e.g., bicycle kick vs Valencia). As a 'Trequartista', he combined prolific goalscoring (GOL, RDS) with high-volume chance creation (KEY, CRK). His lanky, bow-legged dribbling style was deceptive and highly effective. Like many artists, his defensive work rate was minimal (low TPI), but his offensive output (Ballon d'Or '99) was world-class.

Strengths and Weaknesses

Strengths

  • Shooting & Technique (STK): Generational ball-striker; capable of spectacular volleys, bicycle kicks, and free kicks.
  • Dribbling (DXT/FLA): Deceptive, unorthodox, and highly effective 1v1 ability.
  • Creativity & Vision (KEY/CRK): Elite playmaker, created high-volume chances.
  • Goalscoring (GOL): Prolific scorer from midfield/forward roles. (FBref)
  • Big Game Performer (CRS):** Scored in major finals and decisive matches.

Weaknesses

  • Defensive Work Rate (TPI/MRK): Famously low; often criticized for lack of tracking back.
  • Pace (SPR): Relied on skill and timing, not explosive speed.
  • Right Foot: Overwhelmingly dominant on his left foot.
  • Temperament: Could appear moody or disengaged; clashed with managers (Van Gaal).
