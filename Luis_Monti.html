Player Overview

Luis "Doble Ancho" Monti is a legendary and unique figure in football history. He is the only player to have played in two World Cup finals for two different nations: he was a runner-up with his native Argentina in 1930 and a winner with Italy in 1934. He was a "centromediano" (defensive midfielder), the pivot of the 2-3-5 "Metodo" system. Monti was a "ruthless," powerful, and combative player, feared for his tackling and stamina ("Double Wide"). He was a serial winner, capturing 4 Argentine titles with Huracán and San Lorenzo before becoming an icon at Juventus, where he won 4 consecutive Serie A titles.
