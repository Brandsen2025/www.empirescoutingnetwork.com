Quick Summary

A legendary Colombian defender, famed for his incredible pace, agility, and gravity-defying leap. Despite being only 1.73m, he was a dominant, world-class centre-back for Inter Milan for over a decade (455 apps), winning the 2010 Champions League and 5 Serie A titles. He was the victorious captain of Colombia's 2001 Copa América team.

Player Overview

Iván Córdoba was a physical phenomenon who defied the conventions of his position. At just 1.73m, he was one of the shortest centre-backs in world football, yet he compensated with a world-class combination of blistering pace (high PAC) and a legendary vertical leap (high JMP). He was a tenacious, aggressive (high AGG) man-marker and a born leader (high LDR). He captained Colombia to their only major title, the 2001 Copa América, and was a cornerstone of the Inter Milan defense for 12 years, making 455 appearances and winning 15 trophies, including the 2010 UEFA Champions League.

Strengths and Weaknesses

Strengths

  • Pace (PAC): One of the fastest centre-backs in history. Could recover against any striker.
  • Jumping (JMP): A "freakish" vertical leap that allowed him to dominate aerial duels despite his height.
  • Leadership (LDR): A "caudillo." Captain of his country, vice-captain at Inter.
  • Tackling (TKL): A precise, aggressive, and tenacious man-marker.
  • Big Game Player (BGP): Scored the winning goal in the 2001 Copa América final.

Weaknesses

  • Height: At 1.73m, was extremely undersized for a CB.
  • Ball-Playing: A pure defender; not known for his passing or build-up play.
  • Discipline: His aggressive style could lead to fouls.
