Player Overview

Paolo Rossi, "Pablito," was a legend forged in a single tournament. He was not a physical specimen, but a footballing ghost; a striker of supreme intelligence (POI 10.0) and generational poaching instincts (GOL 10.0). He read the game faster than defenders, living on the shoulder and appearing in the right place at the right second. His 1982 World Cup is the stuff of legend: after a slow start, he scored 6 goals in the last 3 games (a hat-trick vs. Brazil) to win the cup, Golden Ball, Golden Boot, and the 1982 Ballon d'Or.
