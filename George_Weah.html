Quick Summary

George Weah is African football's greatest export and an extraordinary rags-to-riches story. From the slums of Monrovia to Ballon d'Or winner, he became the first and only African to win football's highest individual honor (1995). A complete striker combining explosive speed, power, technical brilliance, and clinical finishing, he dominated Serie A at AC Milan. Post-retirement, he entered politics and was elected President of Liberia (2018-2024), completing one of history's most remarkable journeys from football legend to head of state.

Player Overview

George Weah's football journey reads like fiction: born in a Monrovia slum, discovered playing barefoot, rose to win the Ballon d'Or, and eventually became President of his country. On the pitch, he was a phenomenon—a complete striker who combined devastating speed with raw power, technical excellence with ruthless finishing. At 6'1" and powerfully built, he could outmuscle defenders or simply outrun them with explosive acceleration. His dribbling at pace was mesmerizing; he could carry the ball from halfway and beat entire defenses single-handedly. His iconic goal vs Verona (1996), where he ran from his own box to score, epitomizes his extraordinary abilities. Beyond football, he used his wealth to support Liberia, personally funding the national team for years and providing humanitarian aid during civil war, before ultimately serving as President from 2018-2024.
