Player Overview

Obdulio Varela was the heart and soul of Uruguayan football, a dominant holding midfielder and arguably the greatest leader the sport has ever seen. Nicknamed "El Negro Jefe," he was the complete "Number 5" – tenacious in the tackle, intelligent in his positioning, and a commanding presence who willed his teams to victory. He is most famous for captaining Uruguay to the 1950 World Cup, where he orchestrated the "Maracanazo." When Brazil scored in the final, he famously stalled the game by arguing with the referee (a deliberate tactic) to silence the crowd and settle his team. It worked, and Uruguay won 2-1 in one of history's biggest upsets. He also won the 1942 Copa América (named Best Player) and six league titles with Peñarol.
