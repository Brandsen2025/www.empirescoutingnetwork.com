Player Overview

Roberto Carlos da Silva Rocha was a force of nature. He wasn't just a left-back; he was an entire left flank. His game was defined by a unique and terrifying combination of generational pace (SPR 10.0) [cite: 123] and arguably the most powerful shot in football history (STK 10.0)[cite: 129]. He possessed relentless stamina (AQ 9.8) [cite: 124] that allowed him to bomb forward for 90 minutes, delivering goals and assists, while still having the recovery speed to defend. A 3x UCL winner, 2002 World Cup winner, and 2002 Ballon d'Or runner-up.
