Quick Summary

"El Pipita" was one of the most clinical and complete strikers of his generation. A serial winner, he won 3 La Liga titles with Real Madrid and 3 Serie A titles with Juventus. His 2015-16 season for Napoli was legendary, where he scored 36 league goals, tying the all-time Serie A record. A 2014 World Cup finalist with Argentina (75 caps, 31 goals), he was a master of movement, technique, and ruthless finishing, scoring over 300 career goals.

Player Overview

Gonzalo "Pipita" Higuaín is an Argentine-French former professional footballer who is widely regarded as one of the best strikers of his generation. Son of defender Jorge "Pipa" Higuaín, he started at River Plate before a move to Real Madrid, where he won 3 La Liga titles. He achieved legendary status at Napoli, where his 2015-16 season, in which he scored 36 goals to tie the 87-year-old Serie A record, is considered one of the greatest striking campaigns in history. This earned him a €90 million move to Juventus, where he won 3 more Serie A titles. A complete forward, "Pipita" was known for his elite finishing, intelligent movement, physical strength, and technical ability to link play.
