Player Overview

Jari Litmanen is a Finnish footballing icon and one of the most intelligent playmakers of his generation. He spent seven years at Ajax, becoming the creative fulcrum of Louis van Gaal's side that won three consecutive Eredivisie titles and reached two consecutive Champions League finals. His peak came in 1995, when he won the Champions League, the Intercontinental Cup, and finished 3rd in the Ballon d'Or. He was the Champions League top scorer the following season (1995-96) with 9 goals. He later played for Barcelona and Liverpool, though injuries hampered his time at both clubs. He captained Finland from 1996-2008 and is their most-capped player.
