Strengths and Weaknesses

Strengths

  • Pressing Structure: Clear triggers and collective organisation.
  • Youth Development: Proven record of promoting and improving young talent.
  • Transition Play: Rapid switch from defence to attack; clinical on counters.
  • Tactical Adaptability: Adjusts block height & pressing intensity within matches.

Weaknesses

  • Risk Management: High press can be exposed by elite low-block teams with positional patience.
  • Link-up Play vs Deep Blocks: Requires creative midfielders to break compact defences.
  • Load Management: High-intensity model demands rotation to avoid late-season fatigue.
