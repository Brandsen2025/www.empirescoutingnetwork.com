Quick Summary

Jorge "Pipa" Higuaín was a tough, uncompromising Argentine centre-back and the patriarch of a football dynasty. He was renowned for his "Garra" (grit), strength, and leadership at the back. He had the rare and controversial distinction of playing for both of Argentina's giants, Boca Juniors and River Plate. A prolific goalscorer for a defender (59 career goals), he was a hard-tackling, dominant force in the 1980s Argentine league.

Player Overview

Jorge "Pipa" Higuaín was a formidable Argentine centre-back who defined the "enforcer" role in the 1980s. A product of the Nueva Chicago academy, he became a star at San Lorenzo before making a major move to Boca Juniors, where he was a defensive pillar for several seasons. In a highly controversial transfer, he later crossed the divide to join arch-rivals River Plate, where he finished his top-flight career. He was a physically dominant, aggressive, and strong defender who was surprisingly prolific, scoring 59 goals in his career. His legacy continued through his two sons, Federico (a creative #10) and Gonzalo (a world-class #9).
