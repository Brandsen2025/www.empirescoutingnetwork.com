Quick Summary

"El Capitán de América" (The Captain of America), Chumpitaz is one of the greatest defenders in South American history. He was the legendary captain of the iconic **Peru 1970 World Cup team** and the **1975 Copa América-winning** side. A brilliant, elegant, and intelligent sweeper, he was revered for his perfect positioning, clean tackling, and supreme leadership.

Player Overview

Héctor Chumpitaz was the definitive "Captain of America." He was the defensive rock and leader for the greatest generation of Peruvian football, a "Libero" who blended defensive grit with exceptional elegance. His reading of the game was second to none. He captained the dazzling Peru 1970 World Cup team (featuring Cubillas and Sotil) to the quarter-finals and, five years later, was the captain of the **1975 Copa América** champions. He also captained the CONMEBOL All-Star team in 1973. A legend at Universitario (5 league titles) and Sporting Cristal (3 league titles), he is, alongside Elías Figueroa, one of the two pillars of South American defending.
