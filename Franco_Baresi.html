Player Overview

Franco Baresi was the ultimate defensive general, a libero whose influence extended far beyond tackling. His game was built on supreme tactical intelligence (POI), anticipation (INT), flawless positioning, and commanding leadership (CHR). Despite lacking imposing height, he dominated attackers through timing and reading of play (MRK). Technically gifted and composed (CPX, ELE), he could also initiate attacks from deep. He captained one of the greatest club sides in history (Arrigo Sacchi's Milan) and the Italian national team with unparalleled authority.
