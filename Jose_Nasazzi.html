Quick Summary

"El Gran Mariscal" was the legendary captain of the dominant Uruguay team of the 1920s and 30s. He led "La Celeste" to wins at the 1924 & 1928 Olympic Games and the inaugural 1930 FIFA World Cup, where he was named the best player. He is widely regarded as one of the greatest defenders and leaders in history.

Player Overview

José Nasazzi Yarza was the first great captain in World Cup history and the defensive rock of Uruguay's golden age. "El Gran Mariscal" was a "genius defender" who defined the art of leadership, described as a "boss, caudillo, and conductor". He was a complete defender: strong, fast, technically skilled, and a master of positioning. He captained Uruguay to four Copa América titles, two Olympic Gold Medals, and the 1930 World Cup, where he was named the tournament's best player.
