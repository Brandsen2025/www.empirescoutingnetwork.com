Player Overview

Ladislao (László) Kubala was a mythical figure and the man who built FC Barcelona into a global superpower. A refugee who fled Eastern Europe, he was a "total" player 20 years before Cruyff. He had the strength of a "tank," the dribbling skill of an "artist," and was one of the greatest free-kick takers of all time. He led Barcelona to 4 La Liga titles and 5 Copas del Rey. His popularity was so immense that Barça's old stadium (Les Corts) became too small, forcing the club to build the Camp Nou. He is one of the few players to play for three different national teams (Czechoslovakia, Hungary, and Spain). In 1999, he was voted the greatest player in FC Barcelona's history.
