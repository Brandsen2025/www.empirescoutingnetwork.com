Player Overview

Juan Alberto "Pepe" Schiaffino is, without question, one of the greatest footballers of the 20th century. An elegant, "charismatic" and supremely intelligent playmaker, he was the "great leader" of two legendary teams. First, he was the star of Uruguay's 1950 World Cup-winning side, scoring the crucial equalizing goal in the historic "Maracanazo" victory over Brazil. Second, he moved to AC Milan in 1954 and became a club legend, winning three Serie A titles and leading the Rossoneri to their first European Cup final in 1958. He also won 4 Uruguayan titles with Peñarol.
