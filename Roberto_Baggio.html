Player Overview

Roberto "Il Divin Codino" Baggio was a footballing artist, a classic 'Trequartista' (No. 10) defined by sublime technical quality, creativity, and goalscoring (elite DXT, FLA, STK, GOL). His game was built on flawless first touch, elegant dribbling, and 360° vision (KEY, POI). He was a master of free kicks and penalties, and possessed a unique ability to single-handedly decide matches (high CRS), most notably dragging Italy to the 1994 World Cup Final. His low defensive work rate (TPI) often put him at odds with dogmatic coaches, but his offensive genius remains legendary.

Strengths and Weaknesses

Strengths

  • Technique & Dribbling (DXT/FLA): Generational; sublime first touch, close control, elegant dribbler.
  • Creativity & Vision (KEY/CRK): World-class playmaker (Trequartista), saw passes others didn't.
  • Finishing & Set Pieces (STK): Elite finisher (205 Serie A goals), master of free kicks and penalties.
  • Clutch Performer (CRS): Decisive in major tournaments and league matches.
  • Composure (CPX): Calm, graceful style of play (excluding WC '94 final PK).

Weaknesses

  • Work Rate (TPI/MRK): Famously low defensive contribution; clashed with tactical (e.g., Sacchi, Lippi) managers.
  • Pace (SPR): Relied on agility, balance, and intelligence rather than pure speed.
  • Injury Proneness: Suffered serious knee injuries early in his career.
  • Temperament: Sensitive personality; sometimes struggled with managerial conflict.
