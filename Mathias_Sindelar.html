Player Overview

Matthias Sindelar, "Der Papierene," was a footballing artist. He was the captain and creative focal point of the legendary Austrian "Wunderteam" of the 1930s. His nickname, "The Paper Man," was a reference to his slight, frail build, which he used to his advantage, weaving and dribbling through defenses with a "ghost-like" elegance. He was a pioneer of the "False 9" role, a centre-forward who dropped deep to orchestrate play, confusing defenders and creating space. He led Austria to the 1934 World Cup semi-finals. His legend was cemented by his mysterious death in 1939, just months after he famously (and defiantly) scored against and refused to salute the Nazi regime after Germany's annexation of Austria.
