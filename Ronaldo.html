Career Overview

Ronaldo Luís Nazário de Lima, commonly known as Ronaldo Nazário or simply Ronaldo, is a Brazilian football legend and one of the greatest strikers in history. Born on September 18, 1976, Ronaldo was renowned for his explosive pace, exceptional dribbling skills, and clinical finishing ability.

Ronaldo's professional career spanned several top clubs including Cruzeiro, PSV Eindhoven, Barcelona, Inter Milan, Real Madrid, AC Milan, and Corinthians. He won two FIFA World Cups with Brazil in 1994 and 2002, cementing his legacy as a clutch performer on the biggest stages.

Despite battling injuries throughout his career, Ronaldo's talent and impact on the game remain undeniable. He is celebrated for revolutionizing the striker role with his blend of speed, skill, and goal-scoring prowess.
