Player Overview

Domingos da Guia, "O Divino Mestre," was a defender who played like an artist. In an era of "kick and rush" defenders, Domingos was a revolutionary. He was a tall, elegant centre-back who never kicked the ball; he *dribbled* it out of defense. He famously said he "invented the short dribble by imitating the Muidinho, a form of samba." He was a master of the "Drible da Vaca" (a body feint) and played with a supernatural calm. He was the defensive star of Brazil's 1938 World Cup (3rd place) and was named Player of the Tournament at the 1945 Copa América. He is one of the few players to be an idol in three countries, winning league titles with Nacional (Uruguay), Boca Juniors (Argentina), and in his native Brazil (Vasco da Gama, Flamengo).
