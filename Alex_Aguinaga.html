Player Overview

Álex Dario Aguinaga Garzón is widely regarded as the greatest Ecuadorian footballer of all time. He was the quintessential "Enganche" or #10, a cerebral playmaker whose game was defined by "la pausa," sublime vision, and pinpoint passing accuracy. He spent the majority of his career as a club legend at Necaxa in Mexico, making over 500 appearances and winning 3 league titles, 1 CONCACAF Champions' Cup, and finishing 3rd in the 2000 FIFA Club World Cup (beating Real Madrid in the playoff). For Ecuador, he was the long-time captain, earning 109 caps and leading his nation to their first-ever FIFA World Cup in 2002.
