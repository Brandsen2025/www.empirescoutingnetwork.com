Quick Summary

Paolo Guerrero is the greatest striker in Peruvian history and an icon of South American football. A complete #9, he combines immense physical strength, elite hold-up play, and clinical finishing. He famously scored the goal that won the 2012 Club World Cup for Corinthians against Chelsea. He is Peru's all-time top scorer and the captain who led them back to the World Cup after 36 years.

Player Overview

Paolo Guerrero is a warrior-poet of football. Developed at Bayern Munich, he became a Bundesliga regular with Hamburg before returning to South America to become a King. At Corinthians, he scored the header that defeated Chelsea to win the 2012 Club World Cup, the last time a South American team won the trophy. For Peru, he is "El Capitán," the soul of the team. Despite a doping ban controversy, he led his nation to the 2018 World Cup and the 2019 Copa América Final. Even in his late 30s/early 40s, he continued to score for LDU Quito (winning the Sudamericana) and finally returned to his boyhood club, Alianza Lima.
