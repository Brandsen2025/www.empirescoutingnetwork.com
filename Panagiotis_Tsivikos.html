Player Overview

Panagiotis Tsivikos is an emerging talent catching the eye of scouts for his electric style of play. Typically operating as an inverted winger or an advanced playmaker, he possesses the "velcro touch" often associated with elite technical developers. His low center of gravity (AGI: 9.1) allows him to change direction instantly, leaving defenders off balance. While still in the developmental phase regarding physical strength and tactical discipline without the ball, his raw offensive output potential is significant. He mirrors the profile of recent Greek exports like Giannis Konstantelias, prioritizing skill and creativity.

Playing Style & Tactical Profile

Offensive Mechanics

  • Dribbling (DRB: 8.7): Excellent close control; uses body feints rather than just raw speed.
  • Playmaking: Drift inside to find pockets of space; looks for the "killer pass" behind defensive lines.
  • Transition: Highly effective in counter-attacks; carries the ball quickly over distance.
  • Finishing: Prefers curling shots from the edge of the box; composed in 1v1s against keepers.

Development Areas

  • Physical Duel: Needs to build core strength to withstand senior-level physicality.
  • Defensive Workrate: Improving, but requires tactical coaching on pressing triggers.
  • Aerial Ability: Not a target man; relies on ground play.
